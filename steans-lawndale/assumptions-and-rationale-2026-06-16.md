## 1. Trade area demand support

A bottom up build of neighborhood demand, intended to ground the stabilized revenue target in the local trade area rather than in a national benchmark alone. Two independent methods.

**Method A: trade area food spend capture**

| Input | Value | Source / status |
|---|---|---|
| Food at home spend per household | about $3,500/yr | USDA ERS 2024: lowest income quintile spent $5,498 on total food; about 65% at home |
| Trade area households (about 0.75 mi) | about 5,000 | Census ACS; subset of about 14,000 North Lawndale households. Tunable |
| Capture rate (single small store) | 5% to 7% | Conservative for a market where many still drive to larger stores |

Result: 5,000 households x $3,500 x 5% = $700K/yr (about $58K/mo); x 7% = $1.22M/yr (about $102K/mo).

**Method B: sales per square foot cross check**

About 2,200 sq ft of selling area x about $9/wk/sq ft = about $86K/mo. The $9 reflects a small independent grocer in a low income trade area running below the FMI 2024 supermarket benchmark of $18.55/wk per sq ft of selling area.

**Both methods bracket roughly $60K to $100K/mo at retail maturity.** A stabilized target around $80K/mo sits comfortably inside that range and is supported independently by both methods. The point is simply that the ramp target is grounded in the neighborhood.

## 4. Labor capacity and store hours

Replacing the placeholder of 11 hours per day with our actual operating hours model.

**Planned store hours: 7am to 10pm, 15 hours per day.** This is our standard store hours model, and it fits North Lawndale specifically: the neighbors this store serves include early commuters who want coffee and a grab and go before work, and shift workers who get off after 8pm and have nothing open nearby. For a food access store, daily access matters, so the stabilized model assumes 7 days a week, ramping up from a leaner Phase 1 schedule.

**Staffing coverage for the 3,000 sq ft format (Phase 2, stabilized):**

| Daypart | Hours | Staff on shift |
|---|---|---|
| Open (7am to 9am) | 2 | 1 |
| Core day (9am to 6pm) | 9 | 2 |
| Evening peak (6pm to 9pm) | 3 | 2 |
| Close (9pm to 10pm) | 1 | 1 |

This is about **27 labor hours per day, roughly 189 per week** at full stabilization, all at the $18/hr floor plus benefits. Phase 1 runs leaner, with shorter hours and one person on shift through the setup period, and scales to this coverage as retail opens. That reflects staffing ramping over time rather than being fully in place from Day 1, consistent with your note.
