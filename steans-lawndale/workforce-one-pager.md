## What this is

A 3,000 sq ft neighborhood grocery and prepared-food hub in North Lawndale, co-located with Mr. Ed's working commercial kitchen. Phase 1 is a produce-box delivery operation running out of the space (Months 1 to 3). Phase 2 is the full retail buildout (Months 4 to 7). The workforce thesis is the reason this site is different from a typical grocery start-up.

## Three career tracks in one building

Most grocery operators offer one entry-level role at one wage point with no promotion path. Uncle May's at 16th Street offers three:

| Track | Entry | Mid | Lead |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Culinary** (Mr. Ed's kitchen) | Prep Cook, $16/hr | Line Cook, $18/hr | Kitchen Lead, $22/hr |
| **Grocery / Retail** | Clerk, $16/hr | Department Lead, $19/hr | Assistant Store Manager, $24/hr |
| **Logistics** | Driver / Picker, $17/hr | Senior Driver, $19/hr | Fleet Lead, $21/hr |

Year 5 headcount: 14 FTE, roughly 30 percent in mid or lead roles, all promoted from within.

Phase 1 grant ($100K, Months 1-6) and Phase 2 grant ($50K, Months 7-12) align with the two-tranche capital release schedule the model is built around.

## Why this site, why now

- The space at 3726 W 16th is already partly built out (kitchen black iron, refrigeration, counters, shelving in place), which is why we can hit Phase 1 at ~$250K instead of the $930/sq ft Wild Onion build cost.
- Mr. Ed's kitchen is already operating. Culinary hires start producing revenue from Day 1, not after a buildout.
- NLW is in a Low Income / Low Access (LILA) USDA-designated food tract. The retail floor and the delivery operation both serve the same households.
- The workforce commitments above are tied to milestone gates that govern Tranche 2 release. If we miss the workforce numbers in Phase 1, Steans's Phase 2 dollars do not deploy. The accountability is structural, not promissory.
