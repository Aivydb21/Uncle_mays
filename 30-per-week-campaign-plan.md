# Customer Acquisition Campaign: 0 → 30 Orders/Week

**Status:** Draft for Board Review  
**Owner:** CRO  
**Target:** 30 produce box orders/week within 60 days  
**Current Baseline:** 3 orders/week (~$165/week revenue)  
**Required Growth:** 10x orders, 10x revenue

---

## Executive Summary

This campaign pivots Uncle May's from investor outreach to direct customer acquisition. We will drive produce box sales through a multi-channel paid advertising strategy, focusing on Meta (Facebook/Instagram) and Google Ads, targeting Black women ages 25-35 in Hyde Park and surrounding Chicago neighborhoods.

**Campaign Budget:** $2,000/month ($500/week) for 60 days  
**Target CAC:** $15-20 per customer (AOV $35-50)  
**Target ROAS:** 2.5x minimum (breakeven at 1.4x given 35% margins)  
**Campaign Timeline:** 7-day sprint to launch, 60-day optimization cycle

---

## Channel Strategy

### Primary Channels

#### 1. Meta Ads (Facebook + Instagram) — 60% of budget ($1,200/month)

**Why Meta:**
- Targeting precision: Black women, 25-35, Hyde Park + South Side Chicago
- Visual medium perfect for fresh produce imagery
- Instagram Stories/Reels for short-form video content
- Proven platform for local DTC food businesses

**Campaign Structure:**
- **Awareness:** Carousel ads showcasing produce quality, cultural relevance
- **Consideration:** Video testimonials, "unboxing" content, recipe inspiration
- **Conversion:** Dynamic product ads with limited-time offers, free delivery hooks

**Creative Requirements (Advertising Creative owns execution):**
- 15 ad variants across 3 hooks (quality, convenience, culture)
- 5 formats per hook: image carousel, video (15s), video (30s), Stories, Reels
- A/B test: founder-led vs. customer testimonial vs. product-only
- All creative must pass through Advertising Creative before launch

**Targeting:**
- Geo: 10-mile radius from Hyde Park (60637)
- Demo: Women, 25-35
- Interests: Healthy eating, cooking, Black culture, farmers markets, Whole Foods
- Lookalike audiences from existing customer emails (119 Mailchimp + 3 Stripe customers)

**Budget Split:**
- Week 1-2: $150/week (testing, 15 variants live)
- Week 3-4: $200/week (scale winners, kill losers)
- Week 5+: $300/week (full scale on proven winners)

#### 2. Google Ads (Search + Performance Max) — 30% of budget ($600/month)

**Why Google:**
- Intent-driven: people already searching "produce delivery Chicago", "fresh vegetables Hyde Park"
- Lower competition than national grocery brands (local focus)
- Performance Max can auto-optimize across YouTube, Display, Search

**Campaign Structure:**
- **Search Campaigns:** High-intent keywords (produce delivery, CSA box, fresh vegetables Chicago)
- **Performance Max:** Auto-optimized across all Google properties using our creative assets
- **YouTube (optional):** Pre-roll ads for recipe content, testimonials

**Keyword Strategy (RevOps to build, CRO to approve):**
- Primary: "produce delivery chicago", "vegetable box delivery", "fresh produce hyde park"
- Secondary: "csa box chicago", "organic vegetables delivery", "farmers market delivery"
- Negative: "restaurant", "wholesale", "catering" (we're DTC only)

**Budget Split:**
- Search: $300/month (high-intent, conversion-focused)
- Performance Max: $300/month (broader reach, Google auto-optimization)

#### 3. Organic Social (Instagram + Facebook) — 0% paid budget, time investment only

**Why Organic:**
- Build brand trust and community
- User-generated content (UGC) from existing customers
- Repurpose paid creative for organic reach

**Content Calendar (Advertising Creative owns):**
- 3 posts/week: product shots, customer testimonials, recipe ideas
- 5 Stories/week: behind-the-scenes, farm partnerships, delivery day content
- Engage with local food influencers, tag customers who post UGC

### Secondary Channels (Post-Launch, Week 8+)

#### 4. Email/SMS (Mailchimp) — Retention Focus

**Current State:** 3 subscribers (customers cleaned out, need re-import from Stripe)

**Strategy:**
- Re-import all Stripe customers into Mailchimp
- Welcome series for new customers (3 emails over 14 days)
- Weekly newsletter: featured produce, recipes, reorder reminders
- SMS for order confirmations, delivery updates, flash sales (future)

**Owned by:** RevOps (setup), CRO (copy approval via Advertising Creative)

#### 5. Referral Program (Week 8+, if retention is strong)

**Mechanics:**
- Give $10, Get $10 (customer refers friend, both get $10 off next order)
- Track via Stripe promo codes or referral platform (TBD)
- Email + SMS promotion to existing customer base

**Trigger:** Only launch if Week 4-8 shows >30% repeat purchase rate

#### 6. Local Partnerships (Ongoing, opportunistic)

**Targets:**
- Hyde Park coffee shops, yoga studios, bookstores (flyer drops, co-marketing)
- Black-owned businesses on South Side (cross-promotion)
- Churches, community centers (bulk orders, event catering)

**Owned by:** CRO (relationship development), COO (fulfillment coordination)

|---------|---------|---------|-------|-------------|

### ROI Targets

**Assumptions:**
- AOV: $40 (average of $35-50 range)
- Gross margin: 35% ($14 profit per order)
- Target CAC: $15-20
- Breakeven ROAS: 1.4x ($1.40 revenue per $1 spent)
- Target ROAS: 2.5x ($2.50 revenue per $1 spent)

**Success Metrics (60 days):**
- 30 orders/week by Week 8 (240 total orders in 60 days)
- Total revenue: $9,600 (240 orders × $40 AOV)
- Total spend: $4,000
- Campaign ROAS: 2.4x (breakeven+ acceptable for first 60 days)
- CAC: $16.67 ($4,000 ÷ 240 customers)
- Gross profit: $3,360 (240 × $14) - $4,000 spend = -$640 net (acceptable for customer acquisition phase)

**Long-term value:** If 30% of these customers reorder 1x/month for 6 months, LTV = $84/customer, making the $16.67 CAC highly profitable.

## Creative Strategy & Messaging

**Owner:** Advertising Creative (CRO sets strategy, Advertising Creative executes)

### Core Messaging Pillars

1. **Quality Over Convenience**  
   "Fresh produce curated for your kitchen, not mass-market sameness."  
   (Premium positioning, not food desert framing)

2. **Culturally Specific, Not Generic**  
   "Collard greens, okra, black-eyed peas. The produce you grew up with, delivered."  
   (Black cultural relevance without being preachy)

3. **Time Is Money**  
   "Skip the Jewel run. Get Hyde Park's best produce delivered to your door."  
   (Convenience for busy professionals)

### Creative Requirements (Advertising Creative owns execution)

**Meta Creative (15 variants, 3 hooks):**
- **Hook 1: Quality** (5 variants)
  - Carousel: farm-to-door journey, close-ups of fresh greens, chef-quality produce
  - Video: 30s "unpacking your box" with quality callouts
  - Testimonial: customer talking about freshness vs. Jewel/Whole Foods
  
- **Hook 2: Culture** (5 variants)
  - Carousel: culturally specific produce (collards, okra, yams, plantains)
  - Video: 15s Reel "Sunday dinner starts here" (cultural nostalgia)
  - Recipe: "5 ways to use your Uncle May's box this week"

- **Hook 3: Convenience** (5 variants)
  - Carousel: "Your week, simplified" (time savings, no grocery runs)
  - Video: 30s "busy week? we got you" (professional woman, work-life balance)
  - Static: "Free delivery. Zero stress. Fresh produce." (simple, bold text)

**Google Ads Creative:**
- Search ads: text-only, emphasize "Hyde Park delivery", "order today", "fresh produce"
- Performance Max: reuse Meta creative (images + videos), let Google auto-optimize
- Landing page: unclemays.com (current Stripe checkout — audit and optimize in parallel)

**Brand Guidelines (Advertising Creative sets final standards):**
- Tone: Premium but warm, investor-to-investor (not corporate, not overly casual)
- Imagery: High-quality produce photography, real customers (not stock photos)
- Colors: Uncle May's brand palette (TBD by Advertising Creative if not already set)
- No food desert framing, no poverty porn, no "saving the community" messaging

## Conversion Funnel Optimization

**Owner:** CRO (strategy), RevOps (execution), Advertising Creative (landing page copy/design)

1. **Ad Click** → unclemays.com
2. **Landing Page** → Stripe checkout (embedded? hosted?)
3. **Purchase** → Stripe payment confirmation
4. **Fulfillment** → COO handles delivery

## Appendix: Detailed Channel Playbooks

### Meta Ads Playbook

**Campaign Structure:**
```
Account: Uncle May's Produce
├─ Campaign 1: Awareness (Reach objective)
│  ├─ Ad Set 1: Women 25-35, Hyde Park 10mi, Interests: Healthy Eating
│  ├─ Ad Set 2: Women 25-35, Hyde Park 10mi, Interests: Black Culture + Food
│  └─ Ad Set 3: Lookalike 1% (seed: Mailchimp list + Stripe customers)
├─ Campaign 2: Consideration (Traffic objective)
│  ├─ Ad Set 1: Engaged with Awareness ads (retargeting)
│  └─ Ad Set 2: Visited unclemays.com, no purchase (retargeting)
└─ Campaign 3: Conversion (Sales objective)
   ├─ Ad Set 1: Added to cart, no purchase (cart abandonment retargeting)
   └─ Ad Set 2: Lookalike 1-3% (seed: purchasers)
```

**Bidding Strategy:**
- Week 1-2: Lowest cost (let Meta optimize)
- Week 3+: Cost cap at $20 CAC (if we have enough conversion data)

**Tracking:**
- Meta Pixel installed on unclemays.com
- Custom conversions: ViewContent, AddToCart, Purchase
- Offline conversions: upload Stripe purchases weekly for attribution

**Search Campaign Structure:**
```
Campaign: Produce Delivery Chicago (Search)
├─ Ad Group 1: Produce Delivery (broad match)
│  └─ Keywords: produce delivery chicago, vegetable delivery chicago
├─ Ad Group 2: CSA Box (phrase match)
│  └─ Keywords: "csa box chicago", "vegetable box delivery"
└─ Ad Group 3: Hyperlocal (exact match)
   └─ Keywords: [fresh produce hyde park], [produce delivery 60637]
```

**Performance Max Campaign:**
- Asset groups: use all 15 Meta creative variants (images + videos)

- Start with Maximize Conversions (let Google learn)

**Tracking:**
- Google Ads conversion tracking via GA4
- Import Stripe purchases as conversions (RevOps to set up)
