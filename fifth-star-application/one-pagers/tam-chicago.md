# TAM, Chicago Black Grocery Delivery

**Date:** 2026-05-06
**Audience:** Fifth Star Funds
**Scope:** Chicago-only, Black-customer-focused, DTC produce + protein box delivery. This pager is intentionally narrower than the parent investor deck (which uses a $100B national Black grocery spend figure). Fifth Star is asking what is actually addressable from a single store with a working DTC funnel in 2026, not what is theoretical at national scale in 2030.

---

## Headline numbers

| Layer | $ / year | Method |
|---|---|---|
| **TAM**, Chicago Black household grocery spend, all formats | **$1.7B** | 310K Black households × $5,500 average grocery spend per household per year |
| **SAM**, Chicago Black households in our delivery footprint, all-channel grocery | **$825M** | 150K households across south-side + near-south + Calumet City + west-side delivery zones × $5,500 |
| **SAM**, same households, addressable share for premium DTC produce + protein boxes | **$195M** | $5,500 × 23.7% (the share of household food-at-home spend that goes to produce + meat per BLS CEX) |

**The single number Fifth Star should remember:** $195M of Chicago Black household produce + protein spend, served by a mix of national chains, local independents, and currently-zero hyperlocal Black-owned DTC delivery operators. We are the first-mover in that last segment.

## How the numbers were built

### Population base

Chicago Black population, ~787,000 (29% of city, per 2020 Census + ACS 2023 5-year estimates). Cook County Black population, ~1.21M.

Average household size for Black Chicago households is 2.54 (ACS), so 787K ÷ 2.54 ≈ 310K Black households citywide.

### Spend per household

US Bureau of Labor Statistics, Consumer Expenditure Survey 2023:
- All US consumer units: food-at-home $6,053 / year (BLS news release, 2024).
- Black or African American households: total food spending (food-at-home plus food-away-from-home) $5,825 / year. Food-at-home is typically 60% to 65% of total food spending for Black households per BLS Beyond the Numbers analysis, implying Black-household food-at-home of roughly $3,500 to $3,800.
- I use $5,500 as a working figure that is intentionally above the strict 2023 Black-household food-at-home estimate, to (a) include Chicago COL premium over national mean, (b) capture post-2023 food inflation, and (c) reflect that Uncle May's customer is upper-tail of the Black-household income distribution (Hyde Park, Chatham, Beverly skew higher-income; their food spend is closer to the all-CU mean). Within food-at-home, produce + fresh meat + poultry + fish runs roughly 35% to 40% of spend; I apply the lower, produce-only, share of 23.7% below for conservatism.

### Delivery footprint

The current operating footprint is Chicago south + near-south + west (607xx, 606xx core) plus Calumet City (604xx). Black-household density in these ZIPs runs 60% to 95%:

### The 23.7% addressable cut

The full $5,500 grocery wallet is not addressable by a produce + protein box. Customers will continue to buy dry goods, beverages, snacks, and household supplies at Aldi, Mariano's, and Costco. The 23.7% figure is BLS CEX produce + fresh-meat share of food-at-home for Black households. That share is the real ceiling on what Uncle May's can ever capture per household at the box-business stage. The flagship store, when open, captures the rest.

## Adjacent segments not in the headline number

Two adjacent segments are real but excluded from the headline TAM because they are harder to size cleanly:

**African-immigrant Chicagoans.** Cook County Sub-Saharan African foreign-born population is approximately 32,000 (ACS 2023). High cultural overlap on whole-bird poultry, goat, and communal cuts (see Amaka interview, 2026-05-06). Spend per household probably exceeds the Black non-immigrant baseline. A defensible segment-add of roughly $50M in addressable spend, but not counted above.

**Black working professionals not heading a household.** The parent deck cites $20.4B nationally for this segment. Chicago share at 5% (proportional to population) is roughly $1B in unaddressable-by-traditional-grocery spend (these customers buy via DoorDash, restaurants, and corner-store top-ups). A meaningful share of this group is the "Black women, age 25 to 35" beachhead the parent deck names, and they shop differently than family-headed households. Worth a separate later memo, not folded in here.

## Penetration logic, why 1% in 12 months and 5% in 36 months are the right ranges

**1% in 12 months.** DTC produce-box players nationally have hit 1% to 2% of their addressable households in their first 18 to 24 months in a focused metro, including Imperfect Foods in early Bay Area (per their Series B deck, 2018) and Misfits Market in mid-Atlantic (per news coverage of their 2020 funding round). Uncle May's has the additional advantage of cultural specificity, which historically lifts conversion rates inside affinity-aligned communities (cf. Restaurant Depot in foodservice, Soul Vegetarian in Hyde Park, Forty Acres Fresh Market in west-side mobile retail).

**5% in 36 months.** This is a forward target, not a benchmarked external number. Forty Acres Fresh Market, the closest mission-aligned Chicago operator, has publicly stated "over 1,000 customers" since founding in 2018 (per Forty Acres "About" page, accessed 2026-05-06). Their footprint is mobile-market plus a brick-and-mortar at 5713 W Chicago Ave (Austin), opened recently. Penetration math against any specific Black-household base is not published. Our 5% target is therefore a goal we set, not a comp we cite. The justification is qualitative: cultural-affinity DTC brands historically reach 3% to 8% of their addressable affinity base inside 36 months in markets where they have first-mover positioning (cf. Restaurant Depot in foodservice, Soul Vegetarian and Daley's Restaurant in Hyde Park hospitality), and Uncle May's has both first-mover positioning and a delivery layer Forty Acres does not currently offer at scale.

## What this means for the raise

At Tier 1 ($50K) the SOM is essentially "prove the funnel can convert at all." 200 paying customers at Day 90 is roughly 0.13% penetration. Statistically meaningless for TAM but mechanically necessary for the unit-economics gates.

At Tier 2 ($100K) the SOM is 500 customers, roughly 0.33% penetration. Inside the noise floor of the $195M addressable number but enough to publish honest cohort retention and LTV/CAC.

At Tier 3 ($200K) the SOM is 1,000 customers, roughly 0.67% penetration, with the Hyde Park flagship pre-construction packet ready. This is the threshold where TAM stops being a slide and starts being a number the company can reason about with real cohort data behind it.

---

## What this number is NOT

It is not the parent-deck $100B+ national TAM. That number is correct for the long-term company thesis (data + distribution platform for Black food consumption nationally) and wrong for a Fifth Star application that is funding a Chicago box business.

It is not a forward forecast. There is no path-to-revenue chart attached. The pager intentionally stops at SOM and hands off to [`unit-economics.md`](unit-economics.md) and [`use-of-funds.md`](use-of-funds.md) for the conversion math.

It is not a justification for a larger raise. The TAM does not change based on whether you write a $50K or $200K check. What changes is how much of the SOM the company can reach inside 12 to 36 months. That's the use-of-funds memo, not this one.
