# Use of Funds, Uncle May's Box Business

**Date:** 2026-05-06
**Audience:** Fifth Star Funds (F&F equity, Chicago, Black-founder focus)
**Asks:** $50K, $100K, $200K. Each tier is a complete plan, not a partial version of the next.
**Anchored to:** [`unit-economics.md`](unit-economics.md). AOV $90. Contribution margin per order $20.10 (22%). Today's CAC is broken (Pixel feedback loop only fixed 2026-05-05). Target CAC $40 to $55 over 60 days. LTV is unmeasured and buying the time to measure it honestly is a core use of capital at every tier.

A founder note up front: every dollar in this memo is committed at close. None of it is performance-gated by future revenue or subscriber count. Phasing is by construction milestone (supplier onboarded, infra shipped, hire made), not by hitting a metric. That is deliberate. We are asking for capital to find the answer, not capital that arrives only if we already have it.

## Why the larger ask is not just the smaller ask plus more money

Each tier funds a different decision.

- **Tier 1** answers a single question, "is the funnel real." The artifact is an honest CAC number with a working attribution stack behind it. If the answer is no, $50K is the cheapest way to learn.
- **Tier 2** answers two questions in parallel, "is the funnel real" AND "is the customer sticky." Customer stickiness cannot be tested without a community operator in the loop and without enough cohort volume to measure repeat rate against a real denominator. $100K buys both.
- **Tier 3** answers the seed-readiness question, "is the company ready to defend a flagship retail unit on top of a working DTC loop." The Hyde Park pre-construction work has to start in parallel with the DTC proof, not after, because the LOI and the SBA facility have time pressure. $200K runs both tracks at once.

A founder who tells you the larger amount is the smaller amount with more zeros is asking for the smaller amount with more zeros. The plans above are different plans.

## What graduates Uncle May's to a real Seed conversation

From the unit-economics memo's gate table, the seed-readiness criteria are:

1. AOV holds at $75 to $100 (already there, $90 today).
2. Gross margin observed at ≥35% (claimed 40%, requires a per-order COGS audit, funded in all three tiers).
3. CAC at ≤$60 (today's CAC is broken; target is $40 to $55 by Day 60).
4. First-order payback in ≤2 orders (mechanical at target CAC and current CM).
5. 30-day repeat rate at ≥25% (unmeasured today, measurable by Day 60 in all three tiers).
6. LTV/CAC at ≥3.0 (composed from the above, achievable inside Tier 2 or Tier 3).

The founder commitment is concrete: when 4 of these 6 metrics land in their target ranges, Uncle May's is back to Fifth Star (or whoever leads the Seed) for the next conversation, with the data to support it. Until then, the company runs on the capital from this raise and does not draw additional dilution.

## What capital does NOT do at any tier

It does not fund:

- A second city. Chicago is the only market until the flagship is open and the DTC loop is mature.
- Salaries for the founder or any leadership team member. Anthony, Zoe, Jua, Tara, and Matt are uncompensated until the Seed.
- A consumer hardware or app build. The website plus Stripe plus Trigger.dev plus Resend plus Mailchimp plus Apollo plus Canva is the stack for the next 12 months.
- Investor relations agency or PR. Outbound is founder-led.

This is a deliberate constraint. The work the capital must do is acquire customers, prove they stay, and underwrite the flagship, in that order.
