# The Problem

310,000 Black households in Chicago spend approximately $1.7B per year on groceries. Existing options serve them poorly.

- **National DTC produce boxes** (Imperfect, Misfits, Hungry Harvest): national supply, zero cultural specificity.
- **Chain grocery** (Mariano's, Whole Foods): cart breadth without provenance.
- **Discount chains** (Aldi): price floor without quality.
- **Independent grocers** (the African store in the south suburbs): cultural specificity but a 30 to 40 minute drive and no delivery.
- **Forty Acres Fresh Market**, the closest mission-aligned operator, reports "over 1,000 customers" in seven years. The market is wide open.

The job to be done is "deliver culturally-specific produce and protein from named Black-owned farms to my door, at the quality I would drive 40 minutes for, without the drive."

# What We Have Learned and Changed

The catalog pivot, narrated.

Before 2026-04-30 we sold three fixed boxes (Starter, Family, Community). Conversion was poor. Customer interviews and abandoned-cart metadata told us why: customers did not see the protein they wanted (Linda wanted beef short ribs and got a generic box, Lia wanted lamb chops and got a generic box). The fixed-box format hid the SKU choice that customers cared about.

We shipped build-your-own catalog on 2026-04-30. Within 6 days, 2 paid catalog orders (Antoinette $72, Miriam $70). Unit-economics math now reconciles to per-order P&L. AOV is up. Repeat behavior is observable for the first time.

Then Miriam told us we should not have retired the community box, because she feeds two households. We are bringing it back.

**The lesson:** the product was getting in the way of the demand. We were 5 weeks into the box business when we found that out, and 7 days from finding out to shipping the fix.
