## Video 1: **Quality Hook**  
*"Uncle May's brings you the produce your grandmother would pick"*

### Concept
Nostalgia-driven quality story. Show premium produce selection with cultural weight — this isn't generic grocery, this is what *your* grandmother would approve of.

### Visual Sequence (30 seconds)
1. **0-5s:** Close-up hands selecting fresh collard greens at a farm or farmers market (slow motion, golden hour light)
2. **5-10s:** Hands placing greens into Uncle May's branded box, intercut with close-ups of okra, black-eyed peas, yams
3. **10-15s:** Box being delivered to a Hyde Park doorstep (recognizable Chicago residential architecture)
4. **15-25s:** Black woman (25-35, target demo) unboxing, smiling as she inspects produce quality
5. **25-30s:** CTA screen: "Order your first box" + unclemays.com/checkout + Uncle May's logo

### Copy Overlays
- **0-5s:** "Your grandmother knew quality."
- **10-15s:** "Uncle May's brings it to your door."
- **25-30s:** "Order your first box"

### Mood
Warm, familial, premium. Think *Bon Appétit* production quality meets cultural authenticity. No stock footage aesthetic — this should feel hand-selected, intentional.

### Music
Acoustic guitar or light jazz, low tempo, nostalgic but not performative.

---

## Video 2: **Culture Hook**  
*"Fresh, Black-owned, delivered to Hyde Park"*

### Concept
Cultural pride + local community. This is *for us, by us*, rooted in Hyde Park.

### Visual Sequence (25 seconds)
1. **0-5s:** Exterior shot of Uncle May's branding or Hyde Park neighborhood landmark (culturally recognizable)
2. **5-10s:** Montage of culturally specific produce being packed: plantains, collard greens, okra, sweet potatoes
3. **10-15s:** Black business owner (implied Anthony or team) packing boxes with care
4. **15-20s:** Delivery driver (Black, professional) handing box to customer at door, both smiling
5. **20-25s:** CTA screen: "Support Black-owned. Get fresh." + URL + logo

### Copy Overlays
- **0-5s:** "Fresh. Black-owned."
- **10-15s:** "Delivered to Hyde Park."
- **20-25s:** "Order today"

### Mood
Community-forward, pride without preachiness. Clean, modern aesthetic — not rustic/vintage. This is *now*, not nostalgia.

### Music
Contemporary R&B or soul, instrumental, confident tempo.

---

## Video 3: **Convenience Hook**  
*"The produce box that gets our culture"*

### Concept
Time-saving + cultural understanding. Busy professionals don't have to choose between convenience and culture.

### Visual Sequence (20 seconds)
1. **0-5s:** Black professional woman (25-35, business casual) rushing out the door in the morning, phone in hand
2. **5-10s:** Cut to her phone screen: Uncle May's website, 3 clicks to checkout (show UX flow briefly)
3. **10-15s:** Box arrives at her door while she's at work (doorbell cam aesthetic or doorstep shot)
4. **15-18s:** Evening: she's cooking dinner with Uncle May's produce, relaxed, smiling
5. **18-20s:** CTA screen: "Skip the grocery run" + URL + logo

### Copy Overlays
- **0-5s:** "Busy week?"
- **10-15s:** "Uncle May's handles the produce."
- **18-20s:** "Order in 60 seconds"

### Mood
Fast-paced, efficient, modern. Think iPhone commercial energy — sleek, aspirational, *doable*. Not frantic, not stressful. Just *handled*.

### Music
Upbeat electronic or modern pop (clean instrumental), quick tempo.

---

## Brand Guidelines (All Videos)

### DO:
- Show culturally specific produce (collards, okra, plantains, yams, black-eyed peas)
- Feature Black talent (actors, hands, customers) authentically
- Emphasize **premium quality** (close-ups, good lighting, intentional composition)
- Keep it **Chicago-local** (Hyde Park mentions, recognizable architecture if possible)
- Make it **aspirational but accessible** (busy professional who values quality + culture)

### DON'T:
- Use food desert framing ("underserved communities," "food access")
- Show struggle or scarcity narratives
- Use generic stock footage Black families (avoid performative inclusivity)
- Over-explain the cultural specificity (let it be self-evident)
- Make it preachy or educational — this is a produce box, not a documentary

## Success Metrics

These videos will be A/B tested on Meta with the goal of:
- **3-second video plays** (hook effectiveness)
- **Click-through rate** to unclemays.com/checkout
- **Cost per landing page view** (<$2 target)
- **Conversion rate** (orders placed)

Videos should be optimized for **scroll-stop** first (0-3 seconds), then **click** (CTA clarity), then **convert** (trust signals).
