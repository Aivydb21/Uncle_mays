## Tier 1: Required Before First Day of Operations

### 1. Chicago Business License
**Agency:** City of Chicago Department of Business Affairs and Consumer Protection (BACP)  
**Type:** Standard Business License (Retail Merchant)  
**Portal:** [chicago.gov/businesslicense](https://www.chicago.gov/city/en/depts/bacp/sbc/business_license.html) or Chicago Business Center: [businesscenter.chicago.gov](https://businesscenter.chicago.gov)  
**Fee:** $250-$330 (based on revenue; annual renewal)  
**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks after application  
**What you need:**
- Federal EIN (Employer Identification Number)
- Business name and registered agent
- Physical address (3720 W. 16th St.)
- Business structure (C-Corp or LLC — confirm with CEO via UNC-496)
- Owner/officer information (Anthony Ivy + others)

**Action:** CEO or COO to apply online via Chicago Business Center. Gusto can assist with payroll tax registration simultaneously.

### 2. City of Chicago Food Establishment License
**Agency:** Chicago Department of Public Health (CDPH)  
**License Type:** Retail Food Establishment  
**Requirement:** Required for ANY facility that prepares, serves, sells, or stores food for retail sale  
**Portal:** CDPH Business Licensing: [chicago.gov/cdph](https://www.chicago.gov/city/en/depts/cdph.html)  
**Fee:** $330 (annually, based on seating capacity / operation type)  
**Timeline:** 4-6 weeks (includes pre-licensing inspection)  
**What you need:**
- Completed CDPH Food Establishment License application
- Site plan showing food storage, prep, and sales areas
- Proof of food safety certification for the operator-in-charge (see Item 3)
- Evidence of adequate equipment (refrigeration, handwashing sinks, food-safe surfaces)

**Inspection requirements:**
- CDPH inspector will review the physical space
- Space must meet Chicago Board of Health requirements for food retail
- Refrigeration: Commercial-grade refrigeration required for perishables (confirm with Muldrow who provides)
- Handwashing: Dedicated handwashing sink required in prep/storage area
- Pest control: Evidence of pest prevention measures

**IMPORTANT NOTE on Muldrow Space:** The space is currently described as "adjacent retail space" in a pharmacy building. GC needs confirmation that CDPH will license this space for food retail use (distinct from the pharmacy). Some mixed-use buildings require separate licenses for each business unit.

**Action:** General Counsel to confirm with CDPH whether the pharmacy building's configuration supports a separate food establishment license for the adjacent space. Contact CDPH Pre-Application Consultation: (312) 744-5000.

---

### 3. Food Manager Certification (Chicago Required)
**Agency:** Chicago Department of Public Health  
**Requirement:** At least one certified Food Service Sanitation Manager (FSSM) must be on-site at all times during food operations  
**Certification:** Illinois Food Service Sanitation Manager Certification (issued by IDPH-approved providers, such as ServSafe, National Registry of Food Safety Professionals)  
**Fee:** $15 (exam fee) + ~$175 (ServSafe course, if needed)  
**Timeline:** 1-4 weeks (take course + exam)  
**Who needs it:** The person running day-to-day food operations at North Lawndale (GM or designated manager)

**Action:** Identify who will be the Food Service Sanitation Manager for North Lawndale. Enroll them in ServSafe or equivalent certification course immediately. Matt Weschler may have existing certification from Wild Onion Market — confirm and transfer if valid.

---

### 4. Illinois Retail Food Establishment License (IDPH)
**Agency:** Illinois Department of Public Health (IDPH)  
**Requirement:** Required for all food establishments operating in Illinois (separate from Chicago city license)  
**Portal:** IDPH Food & Drugs: [dph.illinois.gov](https://dph.illinois.gov)  
**Fee:** $300-$400 annually (based on establishment size)  
**Timeline:** 4-8 weeks (inspection required)

**Note:** Chicago food establishments typically need BOTH the City of Chicago CDPH license AND the IDPH state license. Both involve inspections (sometimes scheduled jointly, sometimes separately). Confirm with CDPH whether a joint inspection is available.

**Action:** Submit IDPH application simultaneously with CDPH application to avoid sequential delays.

**North Lawndale Zoning Context:**
- 16th St. is a mixed-use commercial corridor (likely B1, B2, or B3 zoning)
- B1/B2/B3 zoning generally allows retail food establishments (produce sales, groceries)
- Pharmacy use is already permitted, so adjacent retail food use should be allowable

**How to verify:** Chicago Zoning Map at [secondcityzoning.org](https://secondcityzoning.org) or DPD Zoning Information Line: (312) 744-5777

**Action:** General Counsel to verify zoning classification for 3720 W. 16th St. within the first week of MOU execution. If zoning requires a special use permit or variance, begin that process immediately (can take 3-6 months for zoning board approval).

**Risk:** If the space requires a zoning variance for food retail use, this could delay operations by 3-6 months. Verify zoning BEFORE investing in equipment or signage.

---

### 6. Illinois Department of Revenue — Retail Registration
**Agency:** Illinois Department of Revenue (IDOR)  
**Requirement:** Register as a retail merchant to collect and remit Illinois sales tax on food sales  
**Portal:** MyTax Illinois: [mytax.illinois.gov](https://mytax.illinois.gov)  
**Fee:** $0 (free)  
**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks  
**Why:** Illinois imposes a 1% reduced sales tax rate on grocery items (under the Illinois reduced-rate food tax), but still requires registration

**Note:** Most grocery/produce items are taxed at 1% in Illinois (not 10.25% Chicago combined rate) because they are "low-rate food" under the state tax code. Confirm which items fall under which rate with CFO to ensure proper POS setup.

**Action:** CFO to complete IDOR registration simultaneous with Gusto payroll setup.

---

### 7. Chicago Cook County Home Rule Sales Tax
**Agency:** Cook County Department of Revenue / Chicago Department of Revenue  
**Requirement:** Collect and remit local sales tax on applicable sales (food items over the state minimum)  
**Rate:** Chicago combined sales tax is 10.25% (state 6.25% + county 1.75% + Chicago 1.25% + RTA 1%); food (grocery) items taxed at 2.25% combined (state 1% + county 1.25%)  
**Action:** CFO to confirm proper POS setup to differentiate taxable vs. reduced-rate food items at North Lawndale location.

## North Lawndale Community Context (Legal/Regulatory Notes)

### Community Development Context
- North Lawndale is a historically underinvested community with active community development organizations (Steans Family Foundation, LISC, etc.)
- **TIF District:** 16th St. / North Lawndale may fall within a Tax Increment Financing (TIF) district — check with City of Chicago Department of Planning and Development. TIF districts can provide grants, loans, or construction subsidies for retail development.
- **SBIF Program:** City of Chicago Small Business Improvement Fund (SBIF) provides grants for facade improvements and interior buildouts in designated TIF areas. If eligible, could offset Phase 2/3 buildout costs.

**Action:** CFO to check if 3720 W. 16th St. is in a TIF district and if SBIF grants are available. This could be a non-dilutive funding source for the buildout.

### Aldermanic Notification
- For any new food retail business in Chicago, it is customary (not legally required) to notify and meet with the local Alderman
- North Lawndale falls under the **24th Ward** (contact: Alderman Monique Scott)
- Community relations with the Alderman's office can accelerate permit approvals and create goodwill
- Uncle May's community focus (Black food economy) aligns with North Lawndale's community development priorities

**Action:** CEO to schedule courtesy meeting with 24th Ward Alderman's office once MOU is executed and operations are confirmed. This is good business practice and may unlock community support.
