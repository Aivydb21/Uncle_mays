## 2. Purpose

Licensor grants Licensee a non-exclusive license to use approximately 3,000 square feet of retail space (the "Licensed Space") for the purpose of operating a produce box delivery business and related food operations.

This Agreement covers an initial 6-month license period, during which Licensee will activate operations, validate demand, and determine whether a long-term lease arrangement is mutually beneficial.

## 6. Infrastructure and Improvements

### Licensor Responsibilities
Licensor agrees to provide the Licensed Space in a condition suitable for food operations, including:
* Functional utilities (electricity, water, drainage, internet) at no additional charge beyond the license fee
* Confirmation that the Licensed Space is zoned for food retail and preparation
* Cooperation with health department inspections and any required landlord certifications

### Licensee Responsibilities
Licensee may install, at its sole expense:
* Refrigeration and freezer equipment
* Shelving, racking, and storage systems
* Food preparation equipment (subject to health department approval)

### Ownership of Improvements
* **Removable Equipment:** Licensee owns all equipment it installs (refrigerators, shelving, etc.) and may remove it upon termination, provided Licensee repairs any damage caused by removal.
* **Permanent Improvements:** Any permanent improvements (e.g., plumbing, electrical upgrades) become property of Licensor upon installation unless otherwise agreed in writing.

### No Joint Funding Commitment
Reference to any third-party funding source (including the Steans Family Foundation or any other grantor) is removed from this Agreement. Each party is responsible for securing its own capital and funding.

## 7. Insurance and Indemnification

  * Illinois Department of Public Health (IDPH) food establishment license

## 9. Termination

### (a) Mutual Termination Right (First 90 Days)
Either party may terminate this Agreement during the first 90 days by providing 30 days' written notice to the other party. No penalty or early termination fee applies.

### (b) Termination for Cause
Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if:
* The other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within 15 days of written notice
* The Licensed Space becomes unsuitable for food operations due to health department action, zoning violations, or unsafe conditions
* Either party ceases operations or becomes insolvent

### (c) Licensor Termination After Month 3
If, at the end of Month 3, Licensee has not demonstrated reasonable progress toward operationalizing the produce box business (defined as: serving at least 10 active customers per week and generating at least $2,000/month in revenue), Licensor may provide written notice of deficiency. Licensee will have 30 days to cure. If not cured, Licensor may terminate this Agreement.

### (d) End of Term (Month 6)
If neither party exercises Option A or B in Section 4, this Agreement automatically terminates at the end of Month 6. Either party may provide 30 days' written notice of intent not to renew.

### (e) Effects of Termination
Upon termination:
* Licensee shall vacate the Licensed Space and remove its equipment within 30 days
* Licensee shall pay any outstanding license fees and repair any damage beyond normal wear and tear
* Licensee retains all rights to the Uncle May's brand, customer data, vendor relationships, and operational systems
* Any prepaid license fees are non-refundable, but Licensee is not liable for future months after termination
