## 1. EMPLOYMENT AND DUTIES

### 1.4 Location
Employee's primary work location shall be Uncle May's Produce Flagship Store, Hyde Park, Chicago, Illinois. Employee acknowledges that the position requires physical presence at the store during operating hours and key business periods.

## 4. CONFIDENTIALITY AND PROPRIETARY INFORMATION

### 4.1 Confidential Information
Employee acknowledges that, in connection with Employee's employment, Employee will have access to and become acquainted with proprietary and confidential information of the Company (collectively, "Confidential Information"), including without limitation:
- Customer lists, customer data, purchasing histories, and customer preferences
- Vendor relationships, pricing agreements, and supplier terms
- Financial information, budgets, forecasts, and business plans
- Operational processes, standard operating procedures, and store layouts
- Pricing strategies, margin targets, and promotional plans
- Marketing strategies, campaign data, and customer acquisition data
- Data products, analytics, and market research related to the Company's data platform
- Personnel information, compensation data, and employee performance records
- Trade secrets, know-how, and technical information
- The Company's expansion plans, site selection criteria, and real estate strategy
- Any other information that is marked "Confidential" or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure

### 4.2 Non-Disclosure Obligation
Employee agrees that during Employee's employment and for a period of **five (5) years** following termination of employment for any reason:
- Employee shall hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and shall not disclose any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the CEO
- Employee shall not use any Confidential Information for Employee's own benefit or for the benefit of any third party
- Employee shall immediately notify the CEO upon learning of any unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential Information

### 4.3 Return of Materials
Upon termination of employment for any reason, Employee shall promptly return to the Company all documents, records, materials, equipment, and other property belonging to the Company, including all copies, regardless of the medium on which such information is stored (paper, electronic, cloud-based, or otherwise). Employee shall not retain copies of any Confidential Information following termination.

### 4.4 Permitted Disclosures
The confidentiality obligations in this Section 4 do not apply to information that: (i) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of Employee; (ii) is rightfully received from a third party without confidentiality restrictions; (iii) is required to be disclosed by law, court order, or government regulation (provided Employee gives the Company prompt written notice and cooperates in seeking a protective order); or (iv) was independently developed by Employee without use of Confidential Information.

### 4.5 Trade Secrets
Employee acknowledges that the Company's Confidential Information includes trade secrets as defined under the Illinois Trade Secrets Act (765 ILCS 1065/1 et seq.) and the federal Defend Trade Secrets Act (18 U.S.C. § 1836 et seq.). Employee understands that misappropriation of trade secrets may subject Employee to civil and criminal liability.

**IMMUNITY NOTICE:** Pursuant to 18 U.S.C. § 1833(b), an individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any federal or state trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that: (i) is made in confidence to a federal, state, or local government official, or to an attorney, solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law; or (ii) is made in a complaint or other document filed in a lawsuit or other proceeding, if such filing is made under seal.

## 6. NON-SOLICITATION

*Note: Illinois Restrictive Covenant Law (820 ILCS 90/) effective January 1, 2022 imposes strict requirements on non-solicitation agreements. Non-solicitation is only enforceable for employees earning ≥$45,000/year as of 2022 (adjusted upward by $5,000 every 5 years). At the $45,000 threshold for 2022-2026, non-solicitation is enforceable if the GM earns ≥$45,000/year, which is confirmed. However, the restrictions must be reasonable in scope and duration.*

### 6.1 Non-Solicitation of Employees
During Employee's employment and for a period of **twelve (12) months** following termination of employment for any reason ("Restricted Period"), Employee shall not, directly or indirectly:
- Solicit, recruit, induce, or encourage any employee of the Company to terminate employment with the Company or to accept employment with any other employer
- Assist any other person or entity in soliciting, recruiting, or hiring any employee of the Company

### 6.2 Non-Solicitation of Customers and Vendors
During Employee's employment and for the Restricted Period, Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit or attempt to solicit any customer or vendor of the Company with whom Employee had personal contact or access to Confidential Information during Employee's employment, for the purpose of providing services or products that are competitive with those offered by the Company.

### 6.3 Non-Compete
*Note: Illinois Non-Compete Law (820 ILCS 90/) effective January 1, 2022 makes non-compete agreements unenforceable for employees earning less than $75,000/year as of 2022 (adjusted upward by $2,500 every 5 years). If the GM earns ≥$75,000/year, a reasonable non-compete is enforceable. If <$75,000, the non-compete clause below is NOT enforceable. CEO should confirm GM salary before including.*

**[CEO: Include this section ONLY if GM base salary is ≥$75,000/year as of 2026]**

During Employee's employment and for a period of **twelve (12) months** following termination of employment for any reason, Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, within Cook County, Illinois and any county contiguous thereto:
- Engage in, own, manage, operate, control, be employed by, provide services to, or participate in any business that directly competes with Uncle May's Produce's grocery retail operations
- "Directly competes" means operating a neighborhood-format grocery store (7,500-12,000 sq ft) with a product mix and cultural positioning substantially similar to Uncle May's Produce

*Note: This restriction is narrowly tailored to Uncle May's specific business model and geographic footprint. A broader non-compete covering all grocery retail would likely be unenforceable in Illinois.*

### 6.4 Reasonableness

### 6.5 Injunctive Relief

Employee therefore consents to the entry of injunctive relief (without bond or other security) to enforce these provisions, in addition to any other legal or equitable remedies available to the Company.

## 7. TERMINATION

### 7.1 Termination by the Company for Cause
The Company may terminate Employee's employment for "Cause" immediately upon written notice. "Cause" means:
- (a) Theft, fraud, embezzlement, or dishonesty involving Company property or finances
- (b) Gross negligence or willful misconduct in the performance of Employee's duties
- (c) Material breach of this Agreement that, if curable, remains uncured 30 days after written notice
- (d) Conviction of, or plea of guilty or no contest to, a felony or any crime involving moral turpitude
- (e) Violation of Company policies involving workplace violence, sexual harassment, or discrimination
- (f) Material misrepresentation in Employee's application for employment or background check

### 7.2 Termination by the Company Without Cause
The Company may terminate Employee's employment without Cause at any time upon **30 days' written notice** (or, at the Company's option, pay in lieu of notice equal to 30 days' Base Salary).

### 7.3 Termination by Employee
Employee may terminate employment at any time upon **30 days' written notice** to the Company.

### 7.4 Termination for Death or Disability
Employment shall automatically terminate upon Employee's death. In the event of a disability that renders Employee unable to perform the essential functions of the position for a continuous period of 90 days or more (with or without reasonable accommodation), the Company may terminate employment upon written notice.

### 7.5 Obligations Upon Termination
Upon termination for any reason:
- Employee shall immediately return all Company property (keys, codes, devices, documents, data)
- Employee shall cooperate with the Company in transitioning responsibilities to a successor
- Employee's obligations under Sections 4 (Confidentiality), 5 (IP Assignment), and 6 (Non-Solicitation) shall survive termination
- The Company shall pay all earned wages through the final day of work (as required by the Illinois Wage Payment and Collection Act, 820 ILCS 115/)
