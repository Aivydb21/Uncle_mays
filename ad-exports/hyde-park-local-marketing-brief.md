## Creative Strategy

### Target Audience
- **Primary:** Black women, ages 25-35
- **Location:** Hyde Park, Chicago
- **Psychographics:** Affluent, health-conscious, value quality and convenience
- **Community:** Culturally connected, word-of-mouth driven

### Positioning
- **Premium neighborhood grocer** (NOT food desert solution)
- **Neighbor-to-neighbor** approach ("Your Neighbor's Produce Box")
- **Convenient and curated** (hand-selected, ready for pickup)
- **Culturally relevant** (imagery and messaging that resonates with Black Chicago)

### Key Messages
1. **Headline:** "Your Neighbor's Fresh Produce Box"
2. **Value Prop:** Hand-curated seasonal fruits & vegetables for your table
3. **Offer:** $30 First Box with code NEIGHBOR20 (save $20)
4. **Location:** Available for pickup in Hyde Park
5. **CTA:** Order Now at unclemays.com

### Design Requirements
✅ QR code prominently placed  
✅ NEIGHBOR20 promo code clearly visible  
✅ $30 First Box offer highlighted  
✅ Hyde Park location mentioned  
✅ Premium aesthetic (deep green #1B5E20, accent green #2E7D32, orange CTA)  
✅ Culturally relevant imagery  
✅ Door hanger: tear-off coupon at bottom with perforated line visual

## Creative Notes

### What's Working
- **Neighbor-to-neighbor messaging** resonates with Hyde Park community culture
- **Premium positioning** matches audience affluence and expectations
- **Tear-off coupon** on door hanger provides tactile, keep-it-handy reminder
- **QR code** bridges physical-to-digital, easy ordering path

### Positioning vs. Competitors
- **NOT Instacart/DoorDash** (delivery markup, no curation)
- **NOT food desert solution** (we serve affluent neighborhoods)
- **MORE LIKE** neighborhood CSA + Whole Foods quality + cultural specificity
- **UNIQUE:** Data-driven curation for Black food preferences
