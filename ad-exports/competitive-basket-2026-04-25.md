## What the data says

1. **Small Box pricing is well-calibrated.** Subscription at $36 is essentially break-even with the same Mariano's basket delivered. One-time at $40 is a modest premium. Hyde Park customers will not see "rip-off" here.

2. **Family Box pricing is the soft spot.** At $70 one-time, a price-conscious comparison shopper sees a ~30–40% premium over a Mariano's organic equivalent delivered. Three options to address:
   - **(a)** Drop Family one-time to **$58–60** (15–22% premium over Mariano's delivered — still defensible). Subscription at $52–54.
   - **(b)** Hold price, ship justifying content (real photos, freshness story, "packed Tuesday morning" vs. Mariano's warehouse-old produce, single vendor accountability) much harder at the moment of decision. Phase 3.2 of the plan was "add 6+ product photos" — that's the prerequisite to defending this premium.
   - **(c)** Reframe the Family Box around protein attach. With the new $15 protein add-on, Family + 1 protein = $85, which is a complete Wednesday-night meal solution — different category than "produce delivered." Lean copy that direction.

3. **The "ugly produce" boxes (Imperfect, Misfits) are not the right comp set.** Their customer is buying surplus rescue produce for $11–15 with an environmental story. Uncle May's customer is buying a curated Black-farmer-sourced premium box. Different psychographic. Don't price against them; you'll lose the brand position.

4. **Demographic question — calibrate or shift?** The Hyde Park median household income (~$75–80k) and the existing presence of Whole Foods + Mariano's premium organic both confirm willingness-to-pay for premium produce in this market. The right answer is **calibrate, not shift.** Drop the Family Box to a defensible premium, or invest in the visual / story justification to hold $70. Shifting to a higher-income segment would mean abandoning the Hyde Park beachhead — premature given we have 3–5 real customers and limited data.

---

## Recommendation

If the `/get-started` A/B test (decision date 2026-05-09) does not show a meaningful CR lift, the next test should be a **Family Box price experiment at $58–60** for two weeks. Hold Small Box at $40/$36 — that's already calibrated. Measure CR + AOV (since Family was the Meta-targeted hero), and decide on permanent reprice based on the resulting unit economics.

In parallel, prioritize getting 4–6 real product photos so the premium can be defended visually, not just on price.
