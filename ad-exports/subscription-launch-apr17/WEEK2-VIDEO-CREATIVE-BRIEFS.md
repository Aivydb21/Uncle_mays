## Variant 1 — Hook Test ("Produce First" or "Price First")

**Hypothesis:** Video 2's format works. Can a different 3-second hook beat 10.7% CTR?  
**Variable under test:** Opening hook only. Everything else mirrors Video 2's format.  
**Length:** 15-20 seconds  

### Two hook options — film both, test both:

**Option A — Produce Hook:**
- [0-3s] Extreme close-up of collard greens, okra, or sweet potatoes. No face. No words. Just the produce.
- Then cut to face: "This is what was in my box this week."
- [3-15s] Walk through what came in the box (pick up each item, name it)
- [15-20s] CTA: "Community Box. $55. Subscribe at unclemays.com — use code FREESHIP."

**Option B — Price Hook:**
- [0-3s] Direct to camera: "Fifty-five dollars a week."
- [3-5s] Pause. Then: "Here's what you get."
- [5-15s] Show box contents one by one — greens, okra, sweet potatoes, other items
- [15-20s] CTA: "Community Box. Subscribe at unclemays.com — use code FREESHIP."

### Shot List
1. Extreme close-up of produce (greens/okra/sweet potatoes) — 0-3s
2. Medium shot (face, setting context) — 3-5s
3. 3-4 close-up cuts (each item from box, pick it up, brief reaction) — 5-15s
4. Close-up (face, direct CTA delivery) — 15-20s

### Props Needed
- Uncle May's box (or any box with produce)
- Collard greens, okra, sweet potatoes (hero items)
- Natural light kitchen or countertop

### File Names (when done)
- `meta_price-hook_vertical_20s_v1.mp4` — Meta Stories/Reels
- `meta_produce-hook_vertical_20s_v1.mp4` — Meta Stories/Reels

## Variant 2 — Testimonial Style (UGC Script 1: Value + Quality)

**Hypothesis:** UGC-style testimonial captures a different audience segment than the product walkthrough. Direct emotional appeal converts at warm/retargeting stage.  
**Variable under test:** Format (testimonial vs. product walkthrough). Hook is secondary.  
**Length:** 20-25 seconds  
**Source:** Script 1 from `ugc-video-creative-briefs.md` — adapted slightly below.

### Script (use as guide, speak naturally — do not memorize verbatim)

**[0-3s] HOOK:**  
*Direct to camera, kitchen or casual background.*  
"I was spending $200 a week at Whole Foods."

**[3-10s] PROBLEM:**  
*Casual, slightly frustrated tone — talking to a friend.*  
"And half the greens would be wilted by the time I got home. I was paying for convenience, not quality."

**[10-20s] SOLUTION:**  
*Pick up collard greens or show the box.*  
"Now I get Uncle May's. $55 a week. Delivered every Wednesday. And the produce? Actually fresh. Like, my-grandma's-garden fresh."

**[20-25s] CTA:**  
*Direct to camera, confident.*  
"Subscribe at unclemays.com. Use code FREESHIP — takes two minutes."

### Shot List
1. Close-up (face, direct to camera) — 0-3s
2. Medium shot (casual setting, arms/hands visible) — 3-10s
3. Close-up (greens in hand, showing quality) — 10-20s
4. Close-up (face, CTA delivery) — 20-25s

### Filming Notes
- Selfie-quality is the GOAL. Do not over-produce this.
- One natural-light location only. Kitchen counter or near a window.
- 3-5 takes — pick the most natural. Do not read directly from the script.
- Pause naturally between the hook and the problem sentence.

### Props Needed
- Collard greens or kale (hero item — hold them up, show them)
- Uncle May's box (background or on the counter)

### File Names (when done)
- `meta_testimonial-value_vertical_25s_v1.mp4` — Meta Stories/Reels
- `meta_testimonial-value_square_25s_v1.mp4` — Meta Feed (crop or reshoot)

## Variant 3 — Box Contents Reveal (B-Roll, No Script)

**Hypothesis:** Pure product reveal with no talking head will perform well at top-of-funnel (cold audience) because it requires zero audio attention and loads intent immediately.  
**Variable under test:** No-person format vs. talking head. Music-driven.  
**Length:** 15-20 seconds  
**Format:** B-roll only, text overlays, background music. No speaking required.

### Shot-by-Shot Direction

**[0-3s] CLOSED BOX:**  
Shot: Uncle May's box sitting on a kitchen counter or table. Static or slow push in.  
Text overlay (center, white, bold): "What's in the Community Box?"

**[3-8s] BOX OPENING:**  
Shot: Hands open the box lid. Slow, deliberate. Camera angle from above (bird's eye) OR 45-degree side angle.  
Text overlay: None. Let the reveal breathe.

**[8-14s] HERO ITEM CLOSEUPS (3 cuts, ~2s each):**  
Cut 1: Collard greens — pick them up, show the fullness of the bunch.  
Cut 2: Okra — hold a few in the hand, fresh and green.  
Cut 3: Sweet potatoes — lay them out on the counter.  
Text overlay on each: just the item name ("Collard Greens" / "Okra" / "Sweet Potatoes") — small, bottom-left, branded font.

**[14-18s] VALUE FRAME:**  
Shot: Full box contents laid out on counter. Wide overhead shot.  
Text overlay (large, bold, center): "Community Box — $55/week"  
Sub-text below: "Delivered every Wednesday to Hyde Park"

**[18-20s] CTA:**  
Shot: Same overhead or slow zoom in on greens.  
Text overlay: "Subscribe at unclemays.com | Code: FREESHIP"

### Music Direction
- Upbeat, soulful — think neo-soul instrumental. No lyrics.
- Reference: anything in the Anthony Hamilton / Soulection aesthetic range.
- Keep it at ~20% volume so any ambient sound still comes through.
- Do NOT use copyrighted music — use a royalty-free track or Instagram's built-in music library.

### Shot List (technical)
1. Static wide shot — closed box on counter — 3s
2. Bird's eye OR 45° — hands opening box — 5s
3. Close-up — greens in hands — 2s
4. Close-up — okra in hands — 2s
5. Close-up — sweet potatoes on counter — 2s
6. Wide overhead — full contents laid out — 4s
7. Slow push-in on greens or box — 2s (CTA screen)

### Props Needed
- Uncle May's box (real or similar)
- Collard greens (fresh, full bunch — hero item)
- Okra (fresh, bright green — at least 8-10 pieces)
- Sweet potatoes (3-4, uniform size)
- Clean counter surface (no clutter — let the produce be the focal point)
- Natural light is ideal (place counter near window, noon to 3pm)

### File Names (when done)
- `meta_box-reveal_vertical_20s_v1.mp4` — Meta Stories/Reels
- `meta_box-reveal_square_20s_v1.mp4` — Meta Feed
