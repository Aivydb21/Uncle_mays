# PRODUCTION BUDGET ESTIMATE

## Option 1: Professional Video Agency

**Per Video:** $2,000 - $5,000  
**Total (9 videos):** $18,000 - $45,000  
**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks (misses April 21 launch)  
**Quality:** High, broadcast-ready  
**Pros:** Professional, polished, high conversion potential  
**Cons:** Expensive, slow, may feel overproduced

---

## Option 2: Stock Footage + Voiceover

**Per Video:** $100 - $200 (stock footage licenses + voiceover)  
**Total (9 videos):** $900 - $1,800  
**Timeline:** 3-5 days (can hit April 21 launch)  
**Quality:** Medium, less authentic  
**Pros:** Fast, affordable, scalable  
**Cons:** Less authentic, stock footage may not match brand

---

## Option 3: In-House Smartphone Production

**Per Video:** $0 - $500 (props, lighting, editing software subscription)  
**Total (9 videos):** $0 - $1,500  
**Timeline:** 5-7 days (tight for April 21 launch)  
**Quality:** Medium, authentic  
**Pros:** Authentic, cost-effective, fast iteration  
**Cons:** Lower production value, requires internal bandwidth

---

## Option 4: Launch Without Video (Static Images Only)

**Cost:** $0  
**Timeline:** Immediate (launch April 21 with static images)  
**Quality:** High (Canva-produced static images)  
**Pros:** Free, fast, professional static creative  
**Cons:** Lower engagement than video, misses 5 video variants from 15 total

---

# RECOMMENDATION

**Phase 1 Launch (April 21):** Use static images only (3 hooks × 1 static variant each = 3 variants ready)  
**Phase 2 (Week 2-3):** Add video variants via Option 2 (stock footage) or Option 3 (in-house smartphone)  
**Phase 3 (Month 2+):** Invest in Option 1 (professional agency) if Phase 1-2 shows strong ROAS

**Rationale:**
- Tight 7-day deadline makes professional video production impossible
- Static images can launch immediately and test hooks
- Add video variants incrementally as budget allows
- Prioritize getting *something* live over waiting for *perfection*
