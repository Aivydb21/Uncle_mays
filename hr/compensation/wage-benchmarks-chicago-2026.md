# Chicago Grocery Retail Wage Benchmarks - 2026

**Research Date:** April 13, 2026  
**Market:** Chicago, IL (Hyde Park)  
**Industry:** Grocery Retail  
**Purpose:** Inform compensation decisions for Uncle May's flagship store hiring

---

## Executive Summary

**Chicago minimum wage (2026):** $16.60/hour for employers with 4+ employees

**Key findings:**
- Uncle May's must pay **above minimum wage** to compete with Whole Foods, Mariano's, and other quality grocers
- Recommended wage premium: **10-20% above market average** to attract top talent for a mission-driven, founder-led startup
- Total store labor cost target: **12-15% of revenue** (vs. industry average 14%)
- Estimated Year 1 total labor cost: **$754K-$936K** for 11 FTE

## Key Compliance Notes

### Chicago Minimum Wage (2026)
- **$16.60/hour** for employers with 4+ employees (effective July 1, 2025, remains in effect for 2026)
- Tipped employees: $12.62/hour
- Youth/transitional employment: $16.50/hour
- Annual increase tied to CPI, capped at 2.5%

**Uncle May's compliance:** All proposed wages exceed $16.60/hour minimum

### Illinois Labor Law
- **One Fair Wage Act:** Tipped employee rules (not applicable to grocery retail)
- **Paid Sick Leave (Chicago):** 1 hour accrued per 40 hours worked, up to 40 hours/year (5 days)
- **Fair Workweek Ordinance (Chicago):** Scheduling requirements for covered industries (may apply to grocery retail with 100+ employees, not Uncle May's initially)
- **Meal and Rest Breaks:** No state requirement, but best practice is 30-min unpaid meal break for 7.5+ hour shifts

**Uncle May's compliance plan:**
- Implement paid sick leave accrual from day 1
- Provide meal breaks for all shifts >6 hours
- Schedule transparency: 2-week advance notice on schedules
