# Payroll Provider Evaluation - Uncle May's Produce
## Illinois Small Business (11-13 Employees)

**Research Date:** April 13, 2026  
**Business Context:** Grocery retail, 11 baseline employees, scaling to 13  
**Location:** Chicago, Illinois (single location initially)  
**Payroll Complexity:** Salaried managers + hourly cashiers/stock team, weekly or biweekly pay

---

## Executive Summary

**Top Recommendation:** **Gusto Simple Plan** ($49/month + $6/employee)

**Why Gusto:**
- Best value for 11-13 employees ($115-$127/month all-in)
- Easy to use for first-time payroll administrators
- Full tax filing and compliance included (Illinois, Chicago, federal)
- Excellent benefits administration (optional health insurance integration)
- Time tracking and PTO management built-in
- Strong customer support and onboarding
- Scales affordably as Uncle May's grows to 82 stores

**Runner-Up:** Square Payroll (if we adopt Square POS)

## Provider Comparison

### 1. Gusto (RECOMMENDED)

**Pricing (2026):**
- **Simple Plan:** $49/month base + $6/employee/month
  - 11 employees: **$115/month** ($1,380/year)
  - 13 employees: **$127/month** ($1,524/year)
- **Plus Plan:** $80/month base + $12/employee/month
  - 11 employees: $212/month ($2,544/year)
  - 13 employees: $236/month ($2,832/year)

**Additional Fees:**
- **State registration:** $200 one-time fee (Illinois setup)
- **Workers comp insurance:** Available through Gusto, but pricing varies (optional)
- **Add-ons:** $10-$30/employee/month for advanced features (optional)

**What's Included (Simple Plan):**
- ✅ Full-service payroll processing (automated tax filing: federal, state, local)
- ✅ Direct deposit for employees
- ✅ Employee self-service portal (W-2 access, pay stubs, PTO requests)
- ✅ New hire reporting (Illinois IDES compliance)
- ✅ Time tracking and PTO management
- ✅ Contractor payments (1099 filing included)
- ✅ Benefits administration (health insurance, 401k integration)
- ✅ Chicago paid sick leave tracking and accrual
- ✅ Multi-state support (if Uncle May's expands to other states)
- ✅ Same-day direct deposit (optional upgrade)
- ✅ Mobile app for employees and admins

**What's NOT Included (Simple Plan):**
- ❌ Dedicated HR support (Plus/Premium only)
- ❌ Compliance alerts and custom reporting (Plus only)
- ❌ Multi-location management tools (Plus only)

**Pros:**
- **Easiest to use** - designed for small businesses, minimal training needed
- **Transparent pricing** - no hidden fees, clear per-employee cost
- **Excellent customer support** - phone, email, chat available
- **Scales with growth** - works for 5 employees or 500
- **Strong benefits integration** - if Uncle May's offers health insurance later, Gusto handles enrollment and deductions seamlessly
- **Time tracking included** - employees clock in/out via mobile app, hours flow to payroll automatically
- **Compliance built-in** - Chicago minimum wage, paid sick leave, tax changes all handled automatically

**Cons:**
- **Price increase (2026):** Base cost rose from $40 to $49/month (22% increase)
- **State registration fee:** $200 upfront to set up Illinois payroll (one-time)
- **Limited HR features on Simple plan** - if Uncle May's needs HR support, must upgrade to Plus ($212/month for 11 employees)

**Best For:**
- Small businesses (5-50 employees)
- First-time payroll administrators
- Companies that want automated compliance and easy onboarding
- **Uncle May's flagship store**

## Illinois-Specific Compliance Requirements

All providers handle Illinois payroll compliance, but here's what Uncle May's must ensure is covered:

### 1. **Illinois Unemployment Insurance (IDES)**
- **Requirement:** Employer must register with Illinois Department of Employment Security (IDES) and pay unemployment insurance tax
- **Rate:** Variable (new employers typically 3.525% on first $13,271 of wages per employee)
- **Compliance:** All providers handle IDES registration and quarterly filings

### 2. **Chicago Minimum Wage**
- **Requirement:** $16.60/hour (2026), indexed to CPI, capped at 2.5% annual increase
- **Compliance:** All providers support custom minimum wage by location
- **Uncle May's action:** Set minimum wage to $16.60 in payroll system, update annually

### 3. **Chicago Paid Sick Leave**
- **Requirement:** 1 hour accrued per 40 hours worked, up to 40 hours/year (5 days)
- **Compliance:** Gusto, Rippling track sick leave accrual automatically; Square Payroll has basic PTO tracking
- **Uncle May's action:** Enable sick leave accrual in payroll system, ensure employees can request PTO via self-service portal

### 4. **Illinois Wage Payment and Collection Act**
- **Requirement:** Employees must be paid at least twice per month (semi-monthly or biweekly)
- **Compliance:** All providers support biweekly or semi-monthly pay schedules
- **Uncle May's action:** Set pay frequency to biweekly (every 2 weeks) or semi-monthly (1st and 15th)

### 5. **New Hire Reporting**
- **Requirement:** Employers must report new hires to IDES within 20 days
- **Compliance:** All providers handle new hire reporting automatically
- **Uncle May's action:** Verify new hire data is submitted when onboarding in payroll system

### 6. **Workers Compensation Insurance**
- **Requirement:** Mandatory for all Illinois employers with 1+ employees
- **Compliance:** Gusto, ADP, Paychex, Rippling offer workers comp insurance (Uncle May's must purchase)
- **Uncle May's action:** Purchase workers comp policy before first employee starts (separate from payroll provider or bundled)

## Recommendation for Uncle May's

### **Primary Recommendation: Gusto Simple Plan**

**Reasons:**
1. **Best value for 11-13 employees** - $115-$127/month all-in, competitive with cheaper options but far more features
2. **Easiest to use** - Anthony, Zoe, or Matt (GM) can administer payroll with zero prior experience
3. **Full compliance included** - Illinois tax filing, Chicago minimum wage, sick leave tracking, new hire reporting all automated
4. **Time tracking built-in** - Cashiers and stock associates clock in/out via mobile app, hours flow to payroll automatically (critical for hourly workforce)
5. **Benefits-ready** - If Uncle May's offers health insurance later, Gusto handles enrollment, deductions, and carrier integration seamlessly
6. **Scales with growth** - Works for 11 employees now, 100 employees at store #5, 1,000 employees at store #30
7. **Strong support** - Phone, email, chat available; excellent onboarding and training

**Trade-offs:**
- $200 one-time Illinois setup fee (acceptable, covers state registration)
- $14/month more than Square Payroll ($115 vs. $101), but Gusto includes time tracking, PTO, and benefits admin that Square lacks

**Setup Timeline:**
1. **Week 0-1 (pre-hire):** Set up Gusto account, pay $200 Illinois registration fee, configure pay schedules
2. **Week 1-2:** Add GM (Matt Weschler) as first employee, run first test payroll
3. **Week 3-8:** Add department leads and managers as they're hired
4. **Week 13:** Add all cashiers and stock associates, enable time tracking on mobile app
5. **Week 16 (store opening):** Full team on payroll, time tracking live, first biweekly payroll runs

---

### **Alternative Recommendation: Square Payroll (IF Uncle May's uses Square POS)**

**Reasons:**
1. **Cheapest option** - $101/month for 11 employees ($1,212/year)
2. **Seamless POS integration** - If Uncle May's uses Square POS, employees clock in/out on the register, hours auto-flow to payroll (zero manual data entry)
3. **Same-day direct deposit included** - Employees can get paid same day (Gusto charges extra for this)
4. **Retail-focused** - Built for stores and restaurants, simple and intuitive

**Trade-offs:**
- **Requires Square POS ecosystem** - Less valuable if Uncle May's uses a different POS (e.g., NCR, Clover, Toast)
- **Weaker HR features** - Basic PTO tracking, no robust benefits admin
- **Less scalable** - Works great for one location, harder to scale to 82 stores across states

**Recommendation:** Uncle May's should choose Square Payroll ONLY if we adopt Square as our POS system. Otherwise, Gusto is the better long-term choice.
