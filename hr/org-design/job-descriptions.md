# 1. General Manager

**Status:** FILLED - Matt Weschler  
**Reports to:** CEO  
**Location:** Hyde Park, Chicago  
**Employment Type:** Full-time, Exempt  
**Compensation:** $60,000-$75,000 + performance bonus

## About the Role

The General Manager is the operational leader of Uncle May's flagship store, responsible for P&L performance, team leadership, vendor relationships, and community engagement. This is a foundational role in a mission-driven company reimagining grocery retail for Black communities. You will build and lead a team of 10-12 employees, drive $6.3M in Year 1 revenue, and help establish the operating model that will scale to 82 stores nationally.

## Key Responsibilities

### P&L and Financial Performance
- Own store-level P&L with full accountability for revenue, margin, and operating expenses
- Achieve Year 1 revenue target of $6.3M ($629/sq ft)
- Maintain 35% gross margin (stabilized) and 15.3% EBITDA margin targets
- Control labor costs to 12-15% of revenue
- Manage shrink and waste to industry-leading levels (<2%)
- Weekly financial reporting to CEO (sales, margin, labor, inventory)

### Team Leadership
- Hire, train, and develop a team of 10-12 employees across all store functions
- Build a high-performance culture aligned with Uncle May's values
- Conduct performance reviews and provide ongoing coaching
- Create staff schedules ensuring optimal coverage and labor efficiency
- Foster a collaborative, accountable, mission-driven team environment

### Operations Excellence
- Ensure exceptional customer experience (target: >90% customer satisfaction)
- Maintain store appearance, cleanliness, and safety standards
- Oversee inventory management and stock replenishment processes
- Implement and refine standard operating procedures
- Ensure compliance with health, safety, and labor regulations

### Vendor and Supplier Relationships
- Build and manage relationships with produce distributors, local farms, and national suppliers
- Negotiate pricing and terms to optimize margin
- Source unique, high-quality products aligned with customer preferences
- Coordinate delivery schedules and manage vendor performance

### Community Engagement
- Represent Uncle May's in the Hyde Park community
- Build partnerships with local organizations, churches, and schools
- Host in-store events and tastings to drive traffic and loyalty
- Gather customer feedback and translate into actionable insights
- Serve as the face of the brand locally

## Qualifications

### Required
- 5+ years of grocery retail management experience
- Proven track record managing P&L for a retail location
- Experience hiring, training, and leading teams of 10+ employees
- Strong financial acumen (margin management, inventory control, labor optimization)
- Deep knowledge of produce quality, handling, and sourcing
- Ability to work 50+ hours/week including evenings and weekends
- Valid driver's license and reliable transportation

### Preferred
- Experience opening or scaling a grocery store from scratch
- Connections to Chicago food distribution networks and local farms
- Background in mission-driven or community-focused retail
- Familiarity with POS systems, inventory management software, and retail analytics tools
- Understanding of Black consumer food preferences and cultural food traditions

## Success Metrics (First 90 Days)

1. **Team built and trained** - All 10-12 positions filled and onboarded before store opening
2. **Store ready** - Inventory stocked, systems operational, compliance complete
3. **Opening day success** - Smooth launch with positive customer feedback
4. **Revenue on track** - Week 1-4 sales trending toward $121K/week target

## Why Join Uncle May's?

- **Mission-driven:** Build something that matters. Uncle May's serves communities that have been underserved by traditional grocers for decades.
- **Ownership:** Full autonomy to build the store operations your way, with support from a world-class leadership team (Booth MBAs, Amazon/Unilever/P&G executives).
- **Growth:** This is the flagship. Prove the model here and you're positioned to lead regional expansion (10-year plan: 82 stores, $625M revenue).
- **Impact:** You're not just managing a store. You're building the infrastructure that will reshape how Black communities access fresh, quality food.

# 2. Assistant Manager

**Reports to:** General Manager  
**Location:** Hyde Park, Chicago  
**Employment Type:** Full-time, Exempt  
**Compensation:** $45,000-$55,000

## About the Role

The Assistant Manager is the GM's right hand, responsible for front-end operations (checkout, customer service) and day-to-day store management. You will lead the cashier team, manage cash operations, handle customer escalations, and serve as acting manager when the GM is off-site. This role requires a customer-first mindset, strong operational discipline, and the ability to keep a fast-paced retail environment running smoothly.

## Key Responsibilities

### Front-End Operations
- Oversee all checkout and customer service operations
- Manage cash handling, drawer reconciliation, and daily deposits
- Ensure fast, friendly, accurate checkout experience (target: <3 min wait times)
- Monitor and optimize front-end labor scheduling
- Maintain clean, organized, well-stocked checkout area

### Team Leadership - Cashier Team
- Hire, train, and develop 4-6 cashier/customer service associates
- Create weekly staff schedules balancing coverage needs and labor budget
- Conduct performance reviews and provide coaching
- Handle disciplinary issues and performance improvement plans
- Foster a positive, team-oriented culture

### Customer Service
- Handle customer complaints and issue resolution
- Process returns, exchanges, and refunds per policy
- Train team on product knowledge and upselling techniques (loyalty program, private label)
- Monitor customer feedback and recommend improvements
- Ensure every customer leaves satisfied

### Store Operations Support
- Serve as acting manager in GM's absence
- Open and close the store per procedures
- Conduct daily walk-throughs to ensure standards are met
- Support inventory counts and audits
- Assist with vendor coordination and receiving as needed

### Financial Accountability
- Daily cash reconciliation and variance reporting
- Monitor cashier accuracy and prevent loss
- Track front-end labor costs vs. budget
- Support GM with weekly financial reporting

## Qualifications

### Required
- 3+ years retail management experience, preferably grocery or food retail
- 2+ years supervising and scheduling hourly teams
- Strong cash handling and POS system experience
- Excellent customer service and conflict resolution skills
- Ability to work 45 hours/week including nights, weekends, and holidays
- High school diploma or equivalent

### Preferred
- Experience with retail scheduling software
- Background in fast-paced, high-volume retail environments
- Bilingual (English/Spanish) a plus for Hyde Park market

## Success Metrics (First 90 Days)

1. **Cashier team operational** - 4-6 cashiers hired, trained, and performing independently
2. **Front-end efficiency** - Average wait time <3 minutes, zero cash shortages >$10
3. **Customer satisfaction** - Positive feedback scores, <5 complaints per week
4. **Labor management** - Front-end labor costs at or below 5% of store revenue

# 3. Department Lead - Produce

**Reports to:** General Manager  
**Location:** Hyde Park, Chicago  
**Employment Type:** Full-time, Non-Exempt  
**Compensation:** $40,000-$50,000 (salary or hourly, TBD based on wage research)

## About the Role

Produce is the heart of Uncle May's. As Department Lead, you own the quality, freshness, and merchandising of the produce department. You will source from local farms and national distributors, ensure every item meets our quality standards, and create a visually stunning department that drives customer loyalty. This role requires deep produce knowledge, an eye for quality, and relationships with Chicago's food distribution network.

## Key Responsibilities

### Quality and Freshness
- Inspect all incoming produce for quality, ripeness, and condition
- Maintain zero tolerance for substandard product on the sales floor
- Implement daily culling and rotation processes (FIFO)
- Monitor temperature and humidity controls for optimal storage
- Reduce shrink and waste to <3% of department sales

### Ordering and Inventory
- Forecast demand and place orders with distributors and local farms
- Manage inventory levels to balance freshness and stock availability
- Build relationships with 3-5 local farms for seasonal, specialty items
- Negotiate pricing and terms to optimize margin
- Track sales trends and adjust assortment accordingly

### Merchandising and Display
- Create attractive, abundant produce displays that drive sales
- Use signage, sampling, and storytelling to highlight local and specialty items
- Ensure all items are clearly priced and labeled
- Rotate displays seasonally to keep the department fresh and exciting
- Collaborate with GM on promotional strategies

### Team Collaboration
- Train cashiers and stock associates on produce handling and customer questions
- Coordinate with Stock team on replenishment and backroom organization
- Support GM with produce-related vendor meetings
- Share knowledge on produce seasonality, cooking tips, and pairings

## Qualifications

### Required
- 3+ years produce department experience in grocery retail
- Deep knowledge of produce quality, seasonality, and handling
- Proven ability to manage inventory and reduce shrink
- Strong vendor negotiation and relationship-building skills
- Passion for fresh, high-quality food
- Ability to lift 50 lbs and stand for extended periods

### Preferred
- Existing relationships with Chicago-area farms and produce distributors
- Experience sourcing specialty or culturally specific produce (African, Caribbean, Southern)
- Background in organic or sustainable agriculture
- Knowledge of food safety regulations (ServSafe or equivalent)

## Success Metrics (First 90 Days)

1. **Department ready** - Produce section fully stocked, displays set, vendors onboarded
2. **Quality standards** - Zero customer complaints about produce quality
3. **Shrink control** - Waste <3% of department sales
4. **Sales performance** - Produce sales meet or exceed $1.8M Year 1 target (30% of store revenue)

# 4. Department Lead - Grocery

**Location:** Hyde Park, Chicago  
**Employment Type:** Full-time, Non-Exempt  
**Compensation:** $40,000-$50,000 (salary or hourly, TBD based on wage research)

## About the Role

The Grocery Department Lead manages center store and dry goods (canned goods, grains, spices, snacks, beverages, household items). You will build the assortment, manage inventory, execute merchandising, and ensure the grocery department is well-stocked and organized. This role requires category management skills, vendor coordination, and an understanding of what products resonate with Uncle May's customer base.

## Key Responsibilities

### Category Management
- Build and maintain assortment across all grocery categories
- Identify high-demand items and optimize shelf space allocation
- Introduce and promote Uncle May's private label products
- Monitor sales trends and adjust assortment to maximize revenue and margin
- Phase out slow-moving SKUs and test new products

### Ordering and Inventory
- Forecast demand and place orders with distributors
- Maintain optimal inventory levels (balance stock availability and cash flow)
- Manage backroom organization and FIFO rotation
- Conduct regular inventory audits and cycle counts
- Coordinate with Stock team on replenishment schedules

### Merchandising and Planogram Execution

- Ensure shelves are clean, faced, and fully stocked
- Create end-cap and promotional displays to drive sales
- Use signage and labeling to highlight deals and new products
- Maintain pricing accuracy across all items

### Vendor Coordination
- Build relationships with grocery distributors and brand reps
- Negotiate terms, promotional allowances, and markdown support
- Coordinate delivery schedules and resolve invoice discrepancies
- Stay informed on new product launches and category trends

## Qualifications

### Required
- 3+ years grocery retail experience in center store or dry goods
- Strong understanding of inventory management and ordering systems
- Experience reading and executing planograms
- Ability to analyze sales data and make assortment decisions
- Detail-oriented with strong organizational skills
- Ability to lift 50 lbs and stand for extended periods

### Preferred
- Experience with private label product management
- Familiarity with culturally specific grocery items (African, Caribbean, Southern cuisine staples)
- Background in category management or merchandising
- Knowledge of POS and inventory management software

## Success Metrics (First 90 Days)

1. **Assortment built** - Full grocery section stocked with optimized SKU mix

3. **In-stock rate** - 98%+ in-stock on core SKUs

# 5. Receiving / Stock Lead

**Reports to:** General Manager  
**Direct Reports:** Stock Associates (1-2)  
**Location:** Hyde Park, Chicago  
**Employment Type:** Full-time, Non-Exempt  
**Compensation:** $38,000-$45,000

## About the Role

The Receiving/Stock Lead is responsible for all inbound logistics, backroom operations, and stock replenishment. You will receive shipments, verify accuracy, organize the backroom, and ensure the sales floor is fully stocked. This role requires strong organizational skills, attention to detail, and the ability to manage a small team of stock associates.

## Key Responsibilities

### Receiving and Inbound Logistics
- Receive all vendor deliveries and verify accuracy against invoices
- Inspect shipments for damage, quality issues, and temperature compliance
- Coordinate delivery schedules to optimize labor and space
- Reject substandard shipments and process vendor credits
- Ensure all receiving documentation is accurate and filed

### Backroom Management
- Organize backroom for efficient storage and FIFO rotation
- Maintain cleanliness and safety standards in receiving and storage areas
- Track backstock levels and communicate replenishment needs to department leads
- Conduct regular inventory audits and cycle counts
- Manage waste disposal and recycling processes

### Stock Replenishment
- Supervise stock associates on daily stocking tasks
- Prioritize replenishment based on sales velocity and out-of-stock alerts
- Ensure shelves are faced, rotated, and organized throughout the day
- Coordinate with department leads on merchandising support
- Monitor and reduce shrink through proper handling and rotation

### Team Leadership
- Train and supervise 1-2 stock associates
- Assign daily tasks and monitor completion
- Provide feedback and coaching on performance
- Foster a safe, efficient, team-oriented work environment

## Qualifications

### Required
- 2+ years receiving or stock management experience in retail or grocery
- Strong organizational and inventory management skills
- Ability to read invoices, packing slips, and track shipments
- Forklift or pallet jack certification (or willingness to obtain)
- Ability to lift 50+ lbs regularly and work in cooler/freezer environments
- High school diploma or equivalent

### Preferred
- Supervisory experience managing 1-3 person teams
- Familiarity with inventory management software
- Food safety certification (ServSafe or equivalent)

## Success Metrics (First 90 Days)

1. **Receiving operational** - All deliveries processed accurately and on time
2. **Backroom organized** - Clean, efficient storage with clear FIFO systems
3. **In-stock performance** - Sales floor 98%+ in-stock on core items
4. **Shrink reduction** - Backroom shrink <1% through proper rotation and handling

# 6. Cashier / Customer Service Associate

**Reports to:** Assistant Manager  
**Location:** Hyde Park, Chicago  
**Employment Type:** Part-time or Full-time, Non-Exempt  
**Compensation:** $17-$20/hour  
**Positions Available:** 4-6

## About the Role

Cashiers are the face of Uncle May's. You create the first and last impression for every customer. This role requires a friendly, efficient, customer-first approach. You will process transactions accurately, answer questions, promote the loyalty program, and ensure every customer leaves satisfied. This is an entry-level role with opportunities to grow into lead or management positions.

## Key Responsibilities

### Customer Checkout
- Greet every customer warmly and create a welcoming environment
- Scan items quickly and accurately using POS system
- Process payments (cash, credit, EBT, mobile payments)
- Bag groceries carefully to prevent damage
- Offer carryout assistance to customers who need it

### Customer Service
- Answer customer questions about products, prices, and store policies
- Handle returns and exchanges per policy
- Resolve minor customer issues independently
- Escalate complex issues to Assistant Manager
- Promote Uncle May's loyalty program and private label products

### Cash Handling
- Maintain accurate cash drawer throughout shift
- Count drawer at start and end of shift
- Follow cash handling procedures to prevent loss
- Report discrepancies to Assistant Manager immediately

### Checkout Area Maintenance
- Keep checkout lane clean, organized, and stocked (bags, receipt paper)
- Maintain impulse purchase displays near registers
- Ensure pricing accuracy and signage is current
- Assist with light cleaning tasks during slow periods

## Qualifications

### Required
- Customer service experience (retail, food service, hospitality)
- Strong communication and interpersonal skills
- Basic math skills and comfort with technology (POS systems)
- Reliable, punctual, and able to work flexible hours (evenings, weekends, holidays)
- Ability to stand for extended periods (4-8 hour shifts)
- High school diploma or equivalent (or currently enrolled)

### Preferred
- Grocery or food retail experience
- Bilingual (English/Spanish)
- Familiarity with POS systems

## Success Metrics (First 30 Days)

1. **Transaction accuracy** - 98%+ accuracy on scanned items and payments
2. **Customer satisfaction** - Positive feedback from customers and managers
3. **Speed** - Process transactions efficiently (target: 20 items/minute)
4. **Reliability** - On-time for all shifts, zero no-shows

## Growth Opportunities

Top-performing cashiers can advance to:
- Lead Cashier (shift supervision, training new hires)
- Customer Service Supervisor
- Assistant Manager (with experience and demonstrated leadership)

# 7. Stock Associate

**Reports to:** Receiving / Stock Lead  
**Location:** Hyde Park, Chicago  
**Employment Type:** Part-time or Full-time, Non-Exempt  
**Compensation:** $16-$18/hour  
**Positions Available:** 1-2

## About the Role

Stock Associates keep the sales floor full, organized, and customer-ready. You will stock shelves, rotate products, assist with receiving, and support department leads with merchandising tasks. This is a physical, fast-paced role that requires attention to detail and a strong work ethic. It's an entry-level position with clear paths to lead or supervisory roles.

## Key Responsibilities

### Stock Replenishment
- Stock shelves and coolers throughout the day based on priority lists
- Rotate products using FIFO method to minimize waste
- Face shelves and organize products for clean, appealing displays
- Check for and report out-of-stock or low-stock items
- Assist with end-cap and promotional display setup

### Receiving Support
- Assist Receiving Lead with unloading deliveries
- Break down boxes and pallets for stocking
- Transport products from backroom to sales floor
- Organize backstock and maintain clean backroom

### Sales Floor Maintenance
- Clean and organize sales floor throughout the day
- Perform price checks and label updates as needed
- Assist customers with locating products
- Report spills, hazards, or maintenance issues immediately

### Waste and Recycling
- Break down cardboard and dispose of waste properly
- Maintain recycling and trash areas
- Ensure compliance with health and safety standards

## Qualifications

### Required
- Ability to lift 50+ lbs and stand/walk for extended periods (6-8 hour shifts)
- Strong work ethic and attention to detail
- Ability to follow instructions and work independently
- Reliable, punctual, and able to work flexible hours
- High school diploma or equivalent (or currently enrolled)

### Preferred
- Grocery or retail stocking experience
- Forklift or pallet jack certification (or willingness to obtain)
- Basic product knowledge (food categories, brands)

## Success Metrics (First 30 Days)

1. **Stocking efficiency** - Complete daily stocking tasks on time
2. **Accuracy** - Proper rotation and placement of products (zero misplaced items)
3. **Reliability** - On-time for all shifts, zero no-shows
4. **Safety** - Zero safety incidents or injuries

## Growth Opportunities

Top-performing stock associates can advance to:
- Stock Lead (supervising other associates)
- Department support roles (Produce, Grocery)
- Receiving Lead
