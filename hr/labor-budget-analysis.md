## Fully Loaded Cost Calculation

### Cost Components

1. **Base Wages:** $415K-$570K (see table above)

2. **Payroll Taxes:**
   - FICA (Social Security + Medicare): 7.65%
   - Federal Unemployment (FUTA): 0.6% on first $7K per employee
   - Illinois Unemployment (SUTA): ~3.0% (new employer estimate)
   - **Total payroll tax rate:** ~10.3-11.3% of base wages

3. **Benefits:**
   - Paid Sick Leave (Chicago ordinance): 1 hour per 40 hours = 2.5% of wages
   - Employee Discount: Margin impact only, not cash cost
   - Health Insurance: Not included in Year 1 benefits package

4. **HR/Admin Costs:**
   - Background checks: $40 × 10-12 employees = $400-$480 (one-time, amortized)
   - Gusto payroll fees: $40/month + $6/employee/month = ~$1,200-$1,400/year
   - Training materials, uniforms, onboarding: ~$200/employee = $2,000-$2,400

5. **Workers' Compensation Insurance:** ~2-3% of wages (Illinois grocery retail rate)

### Fully Loaded Multiplier

Industry standard for retail with basic benefits: **1.38-1.42x base wages**

## Recommendation

**APPROVE Scenario B with contingency: Start with 11 FTE, defer 12th hire to Month 4**

### Rationale

1. **Financial Model Fit:**
   - 11 FTE at $657K = 10.4% of revenue (0.4 points over 10% target)
   - EBITDA margin: 14.9% (vs. 15.3% target) = $27K impact
   - **This is acceptable** given competitive labor market and mission-driven positioning

2. **Wage Band Preservation:**
   - Do NOT reduce posted wage rates
   - Chicago market requires competitive pay for quality hires
   - Mission-driven positioning attracts candidates who value culture, but base pay must be within 10-15% of market
   - Reducing cashier wages to $16.50-$17.50 saves only $20K-$30K but risks higher turnover (cost: $3K-$5K per replacement)

3. **Smart Headcount Phasing:**
   - **Week 0-12:** Hire 11 FTE (5 cashiers, 1 stock associate)
   - **Month 4+ (if needed):** Add 12th FTE (6th cashier) only if:
     - Daily customer traffic > 250/day
     - Checkout wait times > 5 minutes during peak
     - Customer feedback indicates understaffing

4. **Risk Management:**
   - Starting with 11 FTE balances operational needs with financial discipline
   - Store #1 must prove the model works - understaffing (10 FTE) risks poor customer experience
   - Overstaffing (12 FTE) erodes EBITDA by 17% with minimal operational benefit

5. **Strategic Context:**
   - Every percentage point of labor matters for unit economics at scale (82 stores × $63K = $5.2M annually)
   - BUT: Store #1 is proof of concept - customer experience > short-term margin optimization
   - If we under-invest in labor and fail to deliver exceptional service, we risk the entire thesis
