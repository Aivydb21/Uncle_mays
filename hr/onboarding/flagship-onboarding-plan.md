# Uncle May's Produce - Onboarding Plan
## Flagship Store - Hyde Park, Chicago

**Version 1.0**  
**Effective Date:** Store Opening (Week 16 post-funding)  
**Owner:** CHRO + General Manager  
**Purpose:** Ensure every new employee is set up for success from day 1

---

## Onboarding Philosophy

Uncle May's onboarding is designed to accomplish three goals:

1. **Operational readiness** - New hires can perform their job independently by end of onboarding
2. **Cultural integration** - New hires understand our mission, values, and how we work
3. **Early engagement** - New hires feel welcomed, supported, and excited to be here

We differentiate onboarding by role tier:
- **Leadership** (GM, Department Leads, Assistant Manager): 4-week onboarding with strategic context
- **Hourly** (Cashiers, Stock Associates): 2-week onboarding focused on execution

## Week 1: Shadowing and Practice

**Goal:** New hire learns the basics by observing and practicing under supervision

### Daily Structure
- **First 30 min:** Daily huddle with mentor - review today's priorities
- **Core shift:** Shadow mentor, practice tasks with coaching
- **Last 15 min:** Debrief with mentor - what went well, what questions remain

### Role-Specific Week 1 Training

#### Cashiers (Week 1)

**Monday-Tuesday: POS Basics**
- Shadow experienced cashier for 2 full shifts
- Learn how to:
  - Greet customers warmly
  - Scan items accurately
  - Handle cash, credit, EBT, mobile payments
  - Bag groceries (heavy items on bottom, fragile on top, cold with cold)
  - Process common transactions (loyalty program, discounts, coupons)
- Practice: Run 10-20 mock transactions with trainer oversight

**Wednesday-Thursday: Advanced Transactions**
- Shadow assistant manager for complex scenarios:
  - Returns and exchanges
  - Price overrides (with manager approval)
  - EBT transactions
  - Loyalty program enrollment
  - Gift card sales
- Practice: Handle 5-10 real customer transactions with trainer observing

**Friday: Independence Test**
- Work independently on register for 4-hour shift
- Trainer nearby for questions but not shadowing
- End of day: Drawer balancing practice with assistant manager
- **Goal:** 95%+ accuracy on scanned items, zero cash shortages >$5

#### Stock Associates (Week 1)

**Monday: Receiving and Backroom**
- Shadow Stock Lead during morning delivery
- Learn receiving process:
  - Check invoices vs. delivery contents
  - Inspect for damage or quality issues
  - Sign off on deliveries
  - Organize backstock by department
- Practice: Break down 3-5 pallets, organize backroom

**Tuesday-Wednesday: Stocking and Rotation**
- Shadow department leads in produce and grocery
- Learn FIFO rotation (first in, first out)
- Practice:
  - Stock shelves from backroom
  - Rotate older products to front
  - Face shelves (straighten, organize, make it look abundant)
  - Check for out-of-date items
  - Report low stock to department leads

**Thursday: Merchandising and Customer Service**
- Shadow experienced stock associate
- Learn how to:
  - Build end-cap displays
  - Update shelf tags and pricing
  - Assist customers with product location questions
  - Clean and maintain sales floor

**Friday: Independence Test**
- Complete daily stocking tasks independently
- Trainer checks quality at end of shift (proper rotation, neat displays)
- **Goal:** Stock 80% of assigned sections correctly, zero misplaced items

#### Department Leads (Week 1)

**Monday-Tuesday: Department Deep Dive**
- **Produce Lead:**
  - Tour local farms and distributor facilities (if possible)
  - Review opening inventory order with GM
  - Learn quality standards: what to accept, what to reject
  - Set up vendor accounts and ordering systems
- **Grocery Lead:**
  - Review planogram and category assortment with GM
  - Tour distributor warehouse (if possible)
  - Set up vendor accounts
  - Learn inventory management system

**Wednesday-Thursday: Merchandising and Operations**
- Build department displays and set up shelving
- Practice ordering: forecast demand, place test orders
- Learn shrink management: how to track waste, identify causes
- Review department P&L basics with GM

**Friday: First Week Review**
- Meet with GM: Progress check, questions, clarify goals
- Review first vendor orders and delivery schedules
- Finalize department setup plan for Week 2

#### Assistant Manager (Week 1)

**Monday-Tuesday: Front-End Operations**
- Shadow GM on front-end management
- Learn how to:
  - Open and close the store
  - Manage cash operations (safe, deposits, drawer setup)
  - Supervise cashier team
  - Handle customer complaints and issue resolution
- Practice: Run front end for 4-hour shift with GM observing

**Wednesday-Thursday: Labor Management and Scheduling**
- Learn scheduling software (or spreadsheet system)
- Practice building weekly staff schedule:
  - Forecast traffic by day/time
  - Assign shifts to balance coverage and labor budget
  - Ensure compliance (meal breaks, no clopens)
- Review labor cost targets with GM (12-15% of revenue)

**Friday: Systems and Reporting**
- Learn how to pull POS reports (daily sales, top items, hourly traffic)
- Daily cash reconciliation process
- End-of-day checklist and reporting to GM
- First week review with GM

## Week 2: Skill Building and Accountability

**Goal:** New hire operates independently with decreasing supervision

### Daily Structure
- **First 15 min:** Self-check - review tasks, identify questions
- **Core shift:** Work independently, mentor checks in 1-2 times
- **Last 15 min:** Self-assessment - what did I do well, what do I need to improve?

### Role-Specific Week 2 Training

#### Cashiers (Week 2)

**Monday-Wednesday: Efficiency and Customer Service**
- Work full shifts independently
- Focus on speed: target 20 items/minute scan rate
- Practice upselling: loyalty program, Uncle May's private label products
- Handle customer questions independently
- Assistant Manager observes 1x per shift, provides feedback

**Thursday: Problem-Solving Scenarios**
- Practice handling difficult situations:
  - Angry customer (role-play with trainer)
  - Long lines (when to call for backup)
  - Register malfunction (troubleshooting, calling for help)
  - Suspected shoplifting (what to do, what NOT to do)

**Friday: Week 2 Assessment**
- Full day working independently
- Metrics tracked: transaction accuracy, speed, customer feedback
- End of day: 1-on-1 with Assistant Manager
  - Review performance: strengths and areas to improve
  - Set goals for next 30 days
  - Answer questions

**Week 2 Success Criteria:**
- 98%+ transaction accuracy
- Average 18+ items/minute scan rate
- Zero cash shortages >$10
- Zero customer complaints
- Can handle returns, exchanges, and common issues without manager

#### Stock Associates (Week 2)

**Monday-Wednesday: Efficiency and Quality**
- Work full shifts independently
- Focus on speed: Complete daily stocking tasks within shift
- Quality: Proper rotation, neat displays, accurate placement
- Proactive communication: Flag low stock, out-of-date items, display issues

**Thursday: Merchandising and Teamwork**
- Support department leads with end-cap displays and promotions
- Practice setting up new product displays
- Coordinate with cashiers on customer requests ("Where is X?")

**Friday: Week 2 Assessment**
- Full day working independently
- Stock Lead evaluates: speed, accuracy, quality, teamwork
- End of day: 1-on-1 with Stock Lead
  - Review performance
  - Set goals for next 30 days

**Week 2 Success Criteria:**
- Complete 100% of daily stocking tasks on time
- 95%+ accuracy on product placement and rotation
- Zero customer complaints or safety incidents
- Proactively identify and resolve issues (low stock, out-of-date items)

#### Department Leads (Week 2)

**Monday-Friday: Full Ownership**
- Operate department independently
- Place orders, receive shipments, manage inventory
- Build displays, maintain quality standards
- Track department metrics (sales, shrink, margin)
- Train and support cashiers and stock associates on department-specific questions

**Friday: Week 2 Review with GM**
- Review department performance: sales, shrink, customer feedback
- Identify any issues or challenges
- Confirm readiness for store opening
- Set goals for Month 1

**Week 2 Success Criteria:**
- Department fully stocked and merchandised
- Quality standards met (zero out-of-date items, visually appealing displays)
- Vendor relationships established
- First orders placed and received
- Ready to operate independently at store opening

#### Assistant Manager (Week 2)

**Monday-Friday: Full Front-End Ownership**
- Manage front end independently (GM available for questions)
- Build weekly schedule for cashier team
- Conduct daily cash reconciliation
- Handle customer service issues
- Open or close the store 3x this week

**Friday: Week 2 Review with GM**
- Review front-end performance: sales, labor %, customer feedback
- Discuss any challenges or learning gaps
- Confirm readiness for store opening
- Set goals for Month 1

**Week 2 Success Criteria:**
- Front end runs smoothly with minimal GM intervention
- Schedule built correctly (coverage + labor budget)
- Cash operations accurate (zero discrepancies >$25)
- Customer service issues resolved professionally
- Can open and close store independently

## Month 1: Independence and Contribution

**Goal:** New hire operates fully independently and starts contributing ideas

### Week 3-4 (Post-Training)

**All Roles:**
- Work full shifts independently
- Mentor checks in 1-2 times per week (not daily)
- Start contributing ideas: "I noticed X, what if we tried Y?"
- Attend weekly team meetings and participate actively

**Weekly Check-Ins:**
- 15-minute 1-on-1 with manager each week
- Topics: How are you doing? What support do you need? Any feedback for us?

**End of Month 1: 30-Day Performance Review**

**Review Structure (30 minutes):**
1. **What went well:** Celebrate wins and strengths
2. **What to improve:** Specific, actionable feedback on development areas
3. **Questions and support:** What do you need to succeed?
4. **Goals for Month 2-3:** Set 2-3 clear goals
5. **Feedback for Uncle May's:** What could we do better in onboarding or operations?

**30-Day Success Metrics by Role:**

| Role | Key Metrics |
|------|-------------|
| **Cashier** | 98%+ transaction accuracy, 20+ items/min scan rate, positive customer feedback, zero cash shortages >$10, reliable attendance |
| **Stock Associate** | 100% task completion on time, 95%+ rotation accuracy, proactive communication, zero safety incidents |
| **Department Lead** | Department sales on target, shrink <3%, quality standards met, vendor relationships solid, team trained and supported |
| **Assistant Manager** | Front-end runs smoothly, labor costs 12-15% target, cash operations accurate, customer satisfaction high, team morale positive |

**Outcome of 30-Day Review:**
- **Meets expectations:** Continue with ongoing coaching and support
- **Exceeds expectations:** Discuss growth opportunities (e.g., lead cashier, cross-training, advancement path)
- **Needs improvement:** Create 30-day performance improvement plan with specific actions and check-ins

## Month 2-3: Mastery and Growth

**Goal:** New hire is a full contributor, trains others, and identifies process improvements

### Ongoing Development

**All Roles:**
- Mentor newer team members (when Month 2+ hires arrive)
- Contribute to weekly team meetings with ideas and feedback
- Take on stretch assignments (e.g., lead a project, test a new process)

### 90-Day Performance Review

**Review Structure (45 minutes):**
1. **Performance summary:** Review progress over 3 months against success metrics
2. **Strengths:** What are you really good at?
3. **Development areas:** What should you focus on next?
4. **Career goals:** Where do you want to grow at Uncle May's?
5. **Compensation review:** Discuss any raises or bonuses based on performance
6. **Feedback exchange:** How can Uncle May's support you better?

**90-Day Outcomes:**
- **Top performers:** Eligible for raises, promotions, or leadership opportunities (e.g., cashier → lead cashier, stock associate → stock lead)
- **Solid performers:** Continue development, set new stretch goals
- **Underperformers:** Performance improvement plan or separation if no progress since 30-day review

## Continuous Improvement

**After first cohort onboards (Month 1-2 post-opening):**
1. Survey new hires: What worked? What was confusing? What should we add/remove?
2. Survey managers: Are new hires ready? What gaps remain?
3. Update onboarding plan based on feedback
4. Document learnings for future stores (this will scale to 82 locations)

**Onboarding is not static.** It's a living document that improves with every cohort.

---

## Key Success Factors

1. **Mentorship matters.** Pairing new hires with experienced team members accelerates learning and builds culture.
2. **Practice before live.** Cashiers should run 20+ mock transactions before handling real customers. Stock associates should practice rotation before going live.
3. **Frequent feedback.** Daily check-ins in Week 1, weekly in Month 1. Don't wait 30 days to tell someone they're off track.
4. **Culture from Day 1.** Every new hire should understand Uncle May's mission, values, and decision-making framework before they touch a register or stock a shelf.
5. **Independence as the goal.** By end of onboarding, new hires should work independently. If they can't, the onboarding failed—fix it before the next cohort.

**Questions about onboarding?** Contact the GM or CHRO. Onboarding sets the tone for the entire employee experience. Get it right from the start.
