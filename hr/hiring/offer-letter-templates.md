**What's Checked:**

**Process:**

3. CHRO or GM initiates check with provider

5. If concerns arise: consult with CEO before rescinding offer

## I-9 Compliance (Employment Verification)

**Timing:** Must be completed by end of employee's first day of work (no exceptions)

**Acceptable Documents (List A - establishes both identity and work authorization):**
- U.S. Passport or Passport Card
- Permanent Resident Card (Green Card)
- Employment Authorization Document (EAD) with photo

**Acceptable Documents (List B + List C - identity + work authorization separately):**
- Driver's license or State ID (List B) + Social Security card or birth certificate (List C)

**Process:**
1. Employee completes Section 1 of I-9 form on or before first day
2. Employer (GM or CHRO) reviews documents and completes Section 2 within 3 business days
3. Original I-9 forms stored securely (not in personnel file - separate I-9 file)
4. Retain I-9 forms for 3 years after hire OR 1 year after termination (whichever is later)

**Gusto Integration:**
Gusto can facilitate electronic I-9 completion, but physical document review is still required on Day 1.

## Offer Negotiation Guidelines

**When Candidates Negotiate:**

**Salary/Hourly Rate:**
- **Leadership roles:** Up to 5-10% increase from initial offer if candidate has exceptional experience
  - Requires CFO approval for increases >5%
  - Must stay within approved wage bands
- **Hourly roles:** Limited negotiation room (wage bands are already 10-30% above market)
  - Can offer top of range for exceptional candidates
  - Cannot exceed approved wage bands without CFO approval

**Start Date:**
- Flexibility up to 2-4 weeks from initial proposed date
- Must align with hiring timeline (e.g., Department Leads must start 3 weeks before opening)
- GM and CHRO must approve any start date changes

**Benefits:**
- Paid sick leave is legally mandated (not negotiable)
- Employee discount is company-wide (not negotiable)
- Healthcare benefits (if offered in future) would be company-wide

**Other Terms:**
- Schedule flexibility: Limited by operational needs
- Title: Not negotiable (org structure is fixed)
- Reporting structure: Not negotiable

**If Candidate Declines Offer:**
1. Ask for feedback: What would have made you accept?
2. Document reasons in hiring tracker
3. Move to backup candidate within 48 hours
4. Inform GM and CHRO immediately

## Legal Compliance Notes

**At-Will Employment (Illinois):**
- Illinois is an at-will state: either party can terminate at any time, with or without cause
- Include at-will language in all offer letters
- Exception: Employment contracts override at-will (GM may have employment agreement)

**Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO):**
- Uncle May's does not discriminate based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, or veteran status
- Include EEO statement in job postings and employee handbook (not required in offer letters)

**Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA):**
- Exempt (salaried) employees: No overtime, must earn >$35,568/year (2024 federal threshold)
  - GM is exempt (salary >$60K)
  - Department Leads and Assistant Manager: Non-exempt (eligible for overtime) unless salaried >$35,568 and meet duties test
- Non-exempt (hourly) employees: Overtime required for hours >40/week
  - All cashiers and stock associates are non-exempt

**Chicago Minimum Wage (2026):**
- $16.60/hour for employers with 4+ employees
- All Uncle May's wages exceed this threshold
