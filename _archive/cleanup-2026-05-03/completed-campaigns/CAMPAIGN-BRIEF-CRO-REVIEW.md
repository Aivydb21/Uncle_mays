## STRATEGIC RATIONALE

### Why These Hooks Work

**V1 (Social Proof):**  
97% abandonment suggests trust issue. "89% would refer" builds trust before asking for purchase.

**V2 (Cultural Resonance):**  
Target audience is Black women 25-35. "For us, by us" signals this is built for them, not generic grocery.

**V3 (Direct Offer):**  
Price transparency addresses abandonment. "$24" is concrete, "20% off" is abstract. Test both.

**V4 (Problem/Solution):**  
Addresses the "grocery run fatigue" pain point. High shareability = virality potential.

**V5 (Curiosity):**  
Unboxing content performs well on Instagram. "What's in the box?" drives clicks.

**V6 (Testimonial):**  
UGC-style ads outperform branded ads on Meta. Even conceptual testimonial builds trust.

**V7 (No-Risk):**  
"No commitment" directly addresses subscription anxiety (likely abandonment factor).

### Why Price Transparency Matters

Every variant includes either "$24" or "20% off" or both because:
- Traffic is healthy (21 sessions/day) → problem is conversion, not awareness
- 97 abandoned checkouts → likely discovering price later than expected
- Showing price upfront = qualify traffic (only click if interested at that price)
- Reduces checkout surprise = higher completion rate

### Why "No Subscription Required" is Critical

Hypothesis: Many abandonments are people discovering it's a subscription at checkout.

Evidence:
- High abandonment rate (97%)
- Traffic is engaged (reaching checkout)
- Problem is late-stage (checkout, not landing page)

Solution: Address subscription concern in ad copy AND landing page prominently.
