Hi Derek,

Uncle May's Produce is building something I think aligns directly with Black Enterprise's mission around wealth creation and economic participation. We're building the distribution layer for Black food, on both sides of the transaction. We ship produce boxes from Black farmers to Black households today, and we're opening a 10,000 sq ft neighborhood grocery flagship in Chicago's Hyde Park next.

Here is the thesis. Black farmers make up less than 2% of U.S. farms and most lack a reliable channel to the consumers who want to buy from them. Black households spend over $100B a year on groceries from businesses that have not built specifically for them. Uncle May's connects both sides and treats every transaction as a data signal, the first platform built to understand Black food consumption at scale. I'm a Chicago Booth MBA with a PE and M&A background, we have $2M in conditionally approved SBA financing from Busey Bank, the flagship projects $6.3M in Year 1 revenue at 35% gross margins, and the 10-year vision is 82 stores generating $625M in revenue while building wealth in the communities we serve. We won the 2025 TechRise Award and the World Business Chicago Food Innovation Award, and I'm speaking at SXSW 2026.
