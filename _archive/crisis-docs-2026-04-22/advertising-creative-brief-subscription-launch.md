## Business Context

**We are subscription-first, not one-time boxes.** The unit economics only work if customers subscribe:
- **6-month LTV:** $1,264 (blended)
- **Target CAC:** $100
- **Profit per customer:** +$342 over 6 months
- **Payback period:** 1.4 months

**Your job:** Drive subscription sign-ups at <$100 CAC. Every creative variant you ship must sell the **recurring weekly box**, not a one-time purchase.

---

## The Offer

### Three Subscription Tiers
1. **Starter Box** — $30/week (entry point, push toward Community)
2. **Community Box** — $55/week (**default recommendation**, best margin)
3. **Premium Box** — $75/week (aspirational, for families)

### Why Subscriptions Win
- **Convenience:** "Set it and forget it" — fresh produce every week, no reordering
- **Consistency:** "The greens you love, every Thursday"
- **Value:** "Save 15% vs one-time" (if true, confirm with CEO)
- **Cultural fit:** "Your grandmother's pantry, always stocked"

**Creative direction:** Position subscriptions as the obvious default choice. One-time boxes should feel like the exception, not the rule.

---

## Target Audience

### Primary: Black women, ages 25-35, Hyde Park Chicago + surrounding neighborhoods
- **Psychographics:** Affluent, culturally connected, values quality and authenticity
- **Behaviors:** Shops at Whole Foods/Mariano's but wants culturally specific produce
- **Pain points:** "I can't find okra/collards/yams that look like what my mom used to buy"
- **Motivations:** Supporting Black-owned, feeding family well, cultural pride

### Messaging Guardrails
- **YES:** Premium, culturally specific, family tradition, quality, Black-owned pride
- **NO:** Food deserts, charity framing, "help us serve underserved communities"

**We target affluent Black communities who WANT this and CAN PAY FOR IT.** Never position as a social good or food access solution.

## Creative Strategy

### Priority 1: UGC-Style Testimonials (Ship First)
**Why:** Highest trust, lowest production cost, proven to convert in DTC subscription businesses.

**Format:**
- Customer speaks directly to camera (phone selfie quality is fine, authenticity > production value)
- 15-30 seconds
- Vertical 9:16 for Reels/Stories
- Hook in first 3 seconds

**Scripts to test (customer delivers in their own words):**
1. **"I used to spend $200/week at Whole Foods. Now I get Uncle May's for $55 and the produce is actually fresh."**  
   → CTA: "Try your first box free" (if we have a promo) or "Join 500+ Chicago families"

2. **"My kids actually eat their vegetables now. Uncle May's greens taste like my grandma's garden."**  
   → CTA: "Get your first delivery this Thursday"

3. **"I signed up for the Community Box and I'm never going back to the grocery store for produce."**  
   → CTA: "Subscribe in 2 minutes" + show phone completing checkout

4. **"Black-owned, Chicago-based, and they actually know what good okra looks like. I'm obsessed."**  
   → CTA: "Support Black-owned, eat better"

5. **"I thought $55/week was expensive until I saw what I was getting. This is restaurant-quality produce."**  
   → CTA: "See what's in this week's box"

**Sourcing:** 
- Reach out to existing Stripe customers (we have ~10-15 email addresses from past orders)
- Offer $25 credit for a 30-second testimonial video
- Use Mailchimp to send the ask (coordinate with RevOps)

### Priority 2: Product Showcase (Ship Second)
**Why:** Conversion-optimized, shows the value, easy to produce at scale.

**Format:**
- Flat lay of box contents (top-down shot, clean white background or rustic wood table)
- Carousel (3-5 images): Box closed → Box open → Contents laid out → Close-up of hero item (greens, yams, etc.) → CTA card
- Square 1080x1080 for Feed, also crop to 9:16 for Stories

**Copy to test:**
1. **Headline:** "What's in this week's Community Box ($55)"  
   **Body:** "Collard greens, okra, sweet potatoes, heirloom tomatoes, fresh herbs. Delivered Thursday."  
   **CTA:** "Subscribe now"

2. **Headline:** "The produce box Chicago families trust"  
   **Body:** "Fresh, culturally specific, Black-owned. From $30/week."  
   **CTA:** "See all boxes"

3. **Headline:** "Your grandmother's greens, delivered"  
   **Body:** "No more searching 3 stores for good okra. We bring it to your door."  
   **CTA:** "Get your first box"

4. **Headline:** "Farm-to-table, culture-forward"  
   **Body:** "We source from Black farmers. You get restaurant-quality produce. Win-win."  
   **CTA:** "Join Uncle May's"

5. **Headline:** "Premium produce, zero grocery store hassle"  
   **Body:** "Subscribe once, eat well every week. Cancel anytime."  
   **CTA:** "Start subscription"

**Production:**
- Shoot 3-5 box variations (Starter, Community, Premium)
- Refresh weekly with "What's in this week's box" (keeps ads feeling current)
- Canva templates for fast iteration (save as brand templates)

### Priority 3: Founder Story (Ship Third, for Retargeting)
**Why:** Trust signal, brand building, works well for warm audiences who've seen the product but haven't converted.

**Format:**
- Anthony speaking to camera (office or store location)
- 30-60 seconds
- Vertical 9:16 or horizontal 16:9 (both)

**Script outline:**
1. **Hook (0-3sec):** "I left private equity to build the grocery store I wish existed."
2. **Problem (3-15sec):** "I grew up watching my family drive 30 minutes to find good greens. In 2024, that's still happening."
3. **Solution (15-40sec):** "Uncle May's is a data platform and distribution network for Black food consumption. We start with produce boxes because that's where trust is built."
4. **CTA (40-60sec):** "Join 500+ Chicago families. First box ships this week."

**Use case:** Retargeting for people who viewed product page but didn't subscribe.

## Landing Page Strategy (Coordinate with CTO)

**Critical:** All ads must drive to **subscription-optimized landing pages**, not generic homepage.

### Recommended landing page flow:
1. **Hero:** "Chicago's #1 Produce Subscription" + hero image (box contents)
2. **Social proof:** "Trusted by 500+ families" + 3 customer photos
3. **Tier selector:** Default to Community Box ($55/week), Starter and Premium as alternatives
4. **Value props:** 3 bullets (fresh, Black-owned, culturally specific)
5. **CTA (above fold):** "Start Subscription" → Stripe checkout

**A/B test (RevOps runs):**
- Default tier: Community vs Starter (hypothesis: Community default increases AOV)
- CTA copy: "Start Subscription" vs "Get Your First Box" vs "Subscribe Now"
- Hero image: Product shot vs family using produce vs founder photo

### Post-click experience (CTO/RevOps coordinate):
- Stripe checkout must default to **subscription**, not one-time
- Upsell flow: If user selects Starter, show "Most popular: Community Box" before checkout
- Confirmation page: "Your first box ships Thursday" + referral CTA

## Brand Voice & Tone

### Uncle May's Voice
- **Confident, not defensive:** We're premium, we know it, we don't apologize for charging what we're worth
- **Culturally fluent, not performative:** Speak to the audience like family, not like a marketing campaign
- **Founder-led, not corporate:** Anthony's Booth/PE background is a trust signal, lean into it

### Copy Principles
1. **No jargon:** "Produce subscription" not "farm-to-table meal kit solution"
2. **No em dashes:** Use commas, periods, or colons instead (per CRO style guide)
3. **No charity framing:** Never "help us serve," always "join us because this is excellent"
4. **Active voice:** "We deliver" not "Fresh produce is delivered"
5. **Specificity wins:** "Collard greens, okra, sweet potatoes" beats "fresh vegetables"

### Examples of Good vs Bad Copy

**GOOD:**
- "The greens you grew up with, delivered every Thursday."
- "Black-owned, Chicago-based, trusted by 500+ families."
- "Restaurant-quality produce, zero grocery store hassle."

**BAD:**
- "Helping underserved communities access fresh food." (charity framing)
- "A revolutionary farm-to-table experience." (jargon, overpromise)
- "We're disrupting the grocery industry." (corporate, not founder-led)
