# Uncle May's Produce — 90-Day Pre-Opening Timeline

**Purpose:** Master schedule for flagship store build-out and launch coordination. All operational planning (vendor sourcing, hiring, SOP development, budget tracking) anchors to these milestones.

**Timeline Format:** Relative to SAFE funding close date (T+0). Convert to calendar dates once funding closes.

---

## Critical Path Overview

```
T+0  Funding Close → T+14 Permits → T+30 Construction → T+60 Equipment → T+75 Hiring → T+82 Inventory → T+89 Training → T+90 Soft Open → T+97 Grand Opening
```

---

## Phase 1: Funding & Permits (T+0 to T+30)

### T+0: SAFE Funding Close
- **Trigger:** $400K minimum SAFE closes
- **Immediate Actions:**
  - Execute SBA 7(a) loan ($2,007K) with Busey Bank
  - Finalize LOI and lease signing
  - Release deposits to architect (Civic Projects) and designer (Food Market Designs)
- **Owners:** CEO (funding), CFO (SBA coordination)

### T+3: Kick-off Meetings
- **Stakeholders:** Architect, designer, GM (Matt Weschler), landlord, city permit liaison
- **Deliverables:**
  - Final floor plan approval
  - Equipment specifications list
  - Construction schedule from architect
  - Landlord's construction requirements and timeline constraints
- **Owner:** COO

### T+7: Permit Package Preparation
- **Deliverables:**
  - Building permit application (architectural plans, structural calcs, MEP drawings)
  - Health department review submittal
  - Fire marshal review submittal
  - Signage permit application
- **Owner:** Architect (Civic Projects), tracked by COO

### T+14: Permit Submissions
- **Milestone:** All permits submitted to City of Chicago
- **Typical Review Period:** 4-6 weeks (tracked closely for delays)
- **Owner:** COO (permit expediting if needed)

### T+21: Pre-Construction Site Work
- **Actions:**
  - Demolition (if required)
  - Utility rough-ins coordination
  - Landlord TI work completion verification
- **Owner:** GM (Matt Weschler) + COO oversight

## Phase 2: Construction & Equipment (T+30 to T+75)

### T+30: Construction Start (Permit Approval)
- **Trigger:** Building permit issued
- **Duration:** 8-10 weeks typical for 10,000 sq ft grocery build-out
- **Critical Subcontractors:**
  - HVAC (long-lead equipment)
  - Refrigeration (custom walk-ins, display cases)
  - Electrical (power for refrigeration loads)
  - Plumbing (health dept requirements)
- **Owner:** General Contractor (TBD), tracked by COO

### T+30: Equipment Procurement Begins
- **Lead Time:** 60-90 days typical for commercial refrigeration
- **Budget:** Part of $1,460K build-out + $445K soft costs
- **Critical Equipment:**
  - Walk-in coolers/freezers (custom-built, 8-12 week lead)
  - Display cases (produce, dairy, frozen)
  - POS system (hardware + software setup)
  - Shelving, fixtures, carts
  - Back-of-house equipment (prep tables, storage, receiving)
- **Owner:** GM (Matt Weschler) + Food Market Designs (specifications)

### T+45: Mid-Construction Milestone
- **Check-in:**
  - Framing complete
  - MEP rough-ins inspected
  - Refrigeration equipment delivery scheduled
  - Fixture shop drawings approved
- **Owners:** GM + COO

### T+60: Equipment Delivery Window Opens
- **Actions:**
  - Walk-in cooler installation (1 week on-site)
  - Display case delivery and setup
  - POS hardware staging
  - Fixture installation begins
- **Owner:** GM (Matt Weschler)

### T+70: Construction Substantial Completion
- **Milestone:** Certificate of Occupancy (CO) issued
- **Final Inspections:**
  - Building final
  - Health department pre-opening
  - Fire marshal final
  - Electrical/plumbing finals
- **Owner:** COO (permit close-out)

---

## Phase 3: Staffing & Training (T+75 to T+89)

### T+75: Hiring Begins (8 Weeks Before Opening)
- **Positions (Priority Order):**
  1. Assistant Store Manager (Week 1)
  2. Produce Manager (Week 2)
  3. Department leads: Grocery, Dairy, Meat/Seafood (Weeks 3-4)
  4. Cashiers, stockers, prep staff (Weeks 5-7)
- **Target Team Size:** 15-20 FTEs for 10,000 sq ft format
- **Owner:** GM (Matt Weschler) + CHRO support
- **Recruiting Channels:**
  - Indeed (primary)
  - Local community boards
  - Referrals from Matt's network (Wild Onion, prior stores)

### T+82: Initial Inventory Arrives (2 Weeks Before Opening)
- **Budget:** $250K initial inventory (from capital stack)
- **Delivery Sequence:**
  - Week 1: Dry goods, grocery, non-perishables (shelving setup)
  - Week 2: Fresh produce, dairy, refrigerated (final merchandising)
- **Vendor Onboarding Deadline:** Must be complete by T+75 to hit this window
- **Owner:** GM (Matt Weschler) + COO (vendor pipeline)

### T+86: Staff Onboarding (1 Week Before Opening)
- **Training Program:**
  - Day 1-2: Store systems (POS, inventory, safety, health code compliance)
  - Day 3-4: Department-specific training (produce handling, meat safety, cashier protocols)
  - Day 5: Mock opening with friends/family (soft-soft open)
- **SOPs Required by This Date:**
  - Opening/closing procedures
  - Cash handling
  - Receiving protocols
  - Customer service standards
  - Food safety/HACCP
- **Owner:** GM (Matt Weschler) + COO (SOP development)

---

## Phase 4: Launch (T+90 to T+97)

### T+90: Soft Opening (Invite-Only)
- **Audience:** Investors, board, community leaders, press, vendor partners
- **Goals:**
  - Stress-test operations (checkout, stocking, refrigeration)
  - Gather feedback on merchandising, pricing, product mix
  - Identify operational gaps before public launch
- **Owner:** GM + CMO (Tara Weymon, event coordination)

### T+93: Final Adjustments
- **Based on Soft Opening Feedback:**
  - Merchandising tweaks (product placement, signage)
  - Staffing adjustments (add cashiers if lines too long, etc.)
  - Inventory reorders (restock fast movers)
- **Owner:** GM (Matt Weschler)

### T+97: Grand Opening
- **Public Launch:**
  - Marketing campaign (CMO: social, local press, email blast)
  - Grand opening promotions (TBD by CMO)
  - Extended hours first week (capture buzz)
- **Revenue Tracking Begins:**
  - Target: $6.3M Year 1 ($629/sq ft)
  - Weekly reporting to CFO
- **Owner:** GM (operations) + CMO (marketing)
