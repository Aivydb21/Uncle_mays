## THE headline finding — instrument fit, not sector fit, is the real gate

Uncle May's $300K is **preferred equity (8–10% accruing, 3.0–3.2x cash sweep) on a single, SBA-leveraged, cash-flowing operating grocery.** That is a **private-equity / real-asset / impact-yield** instrument — NOT venture-scalable startup equity.

Almost every "Tier 1" name is a **venture or CPG-brand equity fund** that buys priced equity / SAFEs into scalable software or branded products, chasing power-law (10x+) exits. A capped-return yield instrument on one physical store is **structurally off-thesis for those funds — even where the founder/mission fit (Black-founder, food) is perfect.** The funds invest in the *brands sold inside* the store; Uncle May's *is* the store.

**Who actually fits this instrument:** operator-angels who have run physical food/retail, and family offices that speak yield/real-asset natively. The mission/diverse VC funds are better understood as a **future "data & distribution platform" round + warm-intro network**, not buyers of this tranche. The one structurally-correct non-equity path among the mission set is the **New Voices Foundation** non-dilutive grant track.

> ### ⬆️ ADDENDUM — CEO reframe (2026-06-08, supersedes the conclusion above)
> Anthony: the single store is **round one of a multi-round structure** toward the 82-store national platform. Reaching an A round marks up the early holder = the 10x leg, same dynamic as any venture bet. The asset's SBA-amortizing cash flow + preferred coupon floors a holder at **~4x** even if the scale hypothesis fails. So the security is **venture upside with a structured downside floor — capped downside, uncapped upside** (a *better* risk shape than vanilla venture), NOT a capped-yield real-asset instrument.
>
> **This reverses the "instrument knockout"** for every fund benched purely on structure. Re-opened (their thesis IS the scale story; floor de-risks the bet): **Supply Change (Cadena), Chingona, Serena, New Voices, Barrel (Cooper), Springdale (Gilbreath), Color (Cantino), Slauson.** Conditional-in on the data-platform framing: **Kapor** (tech-driven filter) and **FTW (Frank)** (food-tech).
>
> **Still out regardless of structure** (thesis/geo/activity, not instrument): Dipalo (hard-tech pivot), Rev1 (Ohio mandate), Homeroom (dead), Debut (dormant — verify), Fearless (legal — verify), Listen (check floor > current round; re-engage at the platform round).
>
> **CRUX / open condition:** the 10x leg only exists if the security *participates in the markup* — i.e., converts / carries conversion rights into later priced rounds (SAFE / convertible / participating preferred). A preferred hard-capped at a 3.0–3.2x cash sweep tops out at ~3.2x and a venture fund cannot fund-return on it. CLAUDE.md lists a **SAFE ($5M cap, 20% discount)** that *does* convert; the close-plan lists the **capped preferred**. **Confirm which security this cohort is offered** (SAFE / preferred / hybrid floored ~4x + converting) — it decides whether the venture-leg funds are truly in play.
>
> **Resulting shape:** a **barbell** final 20 — upside-leg mission/data/food-VC funds (pitch: scale + diversity, floor as de-risk) + downside-leg operator-angels & family offices (pitch: cash-flowing floored asset, scale as free option).
