## Deal frame (screening anchor)

- **Remaining raise:** ~$300K of the $400K preferred tranche (Reggie Binford in at $100K lead).
- **Check size:** floor **$25K**, ceiling **$300K** (a single $300K check could complete the round).
- **Structure:** $2.0M SBA 7(a) senior (Busey, conditional) + $400K preferred at 8–10% accruing, 3.0x–3.2x cash sweep. Single-asset Hyde Park flagship, $8M+ existing revenue, GM Matt Weschler (two prior Chicago grocery exits). 18.4% five-year IRR, 23.3% at ten.
- **This is NOT a venture deal.** It is a cash-flowing, SBA-leveraged operating grocery. That reframe is what filters a venture database correctly.

## Target archetypes (who survives)

1. **Operator-angels** — grocery / retail / CPG / restaurant founders who write their own checks.
2. **Family offices** with flexible check sizes and operating-asset comfort.
3. **Food-systems / food-access impact funds** (and diverse/mission-aligned funds).
4. **ETA / self-funded-searcher capital** comfortable with SBA-plus-equity stacks.

## Knockouts (Gate 1)

Non-US · typical check too big to fit a ≤~$300K round · mandate excludes the structure (pure SaaS / biotech / late-stage / power-law venture) · no food/retail/consumer/impact tag · inactive >18 mo · **Liz Abunaw / Forty Acres Fresh Market (hard exclude — never propose outreach; citing as published comp is fine).**

## Scoring rubric (applied to survivors at final cut)

Access **0–5** (weighted highest) · Active deployment **0–3** · Model understanding **0–3** · Value-add **0–2** · Mission **0–2** = **/15**. Sort, draw the line at the top 20. High-fit / no-access names go to an "intro-path bench."
