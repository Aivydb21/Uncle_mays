*MISSION*

## Why This Matters

This is more than a grocery business. It is the channel layer for a Black food economy that has supply but no scaled distribution.

- **Reconnecting Black consumers with Black agriculture.** Black farmers represent less than 2% of U.S. agricultural producers (USDA 2022). The supply exists; the channel does not.
- **Closing a food access gap across the South and West Sides.** Customers in our pilot describe their current options in their own words as "hunting for food."
- **Supporting Black farm economics.** Every order routes dollars directly to Black-owned farms instead of through national distributors.
- **Convenience without sacrificing identity.** Customers no longer choose between cultural alignment and grocery convenience.
- **Rebuilding local food trust.** Named farms, named neighborhoods, named delivery days. No anonymity in the supply chain.

*OPERATING MODEL*

## How Uncle May's Works

A simple, transparent operating flow.

**Step 1: Customer orders online by Sunday night.**
Build-your-own catalog. Customer chooses produce and proteins. No subscription, no minimum, no contract.

**Step 2: Orders are aggregated with Black-owned farm partners.**
Anchor supplier today: Run A Way Buckers Club, Pembroke, IL. Additional Black-owned suppliers added as catalog expands.

**Step 3: Produce and proteins are packed in Chicago.**
Hand-packed for each customer. Quality-checked. Branded packaging.

**Step 4: Wednesday delivery across Chicago neighborhoods.**
South Side, West Side, North Side, near suburbs. Customers see who is delivering and what they ordered.

**Result:** Fresh, culturally relevant groceries delivered conveniently while supporting Black farmers.

*ADAPTABILITY*

## What We Learned and Changed

*The catalog pivot, told briefly.*

Before April 30 we sold three fixed boxes (Starter, Family, Community). Conversion was poor. Customer interviews and abandoned-cart data showed why: customers could not see the proteins they actually wanted. Linda wanted beef short ribs and saw a generic box. Lia wanted lamb chops and saw a generic box.

We shipped a build-your-own catalog on April 30. Within a week, paid catalog orders began arriving with the proteins customers had been asking for.

Then Miriam told us we should not have retired the community box because she feeds two households. We are bringing it back.

**The lesson:** the product was getting in the way of the demand. We were five weeks into the box business when we found that out, and seven days from finding out to shipping the fix.

*UNIT ECONOMICS*

## Unit Economics

**Per-order economics**

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Observed AOV (cleaned, 4 orders) | $68 |
| Target AOV (6-month) | $75 to $100 |
| Gross margin (target) | 40% ($30 to $36 per order) |
| Contribution margin (target) | 22% of AOV ($17 to $20 per order) |
| Target customer acquisition cost | $40 to $55 |

**Three CAC scenarios**

| Scenario | CAC | First-order payback | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Today (observed) | ~$270 | ~13 orders to recover | $797 lifetime paid spend across Meta + Google, 3 platform-attributed purchases. Pre-fix Pixel feedback loop and pre-catalog era are both inside this number. |
| Target (6-month) | $40 to $55 | First order net-positive | Reasonable for Chicago hyperlocal Meta + Google with geo gates, negative keywords, and creative aligned to /shop. Anchored to consumer-perishables benchmarks in mid-tier markets. |
| Required for venture payback | Below $25 | Payback in one order | Achievable only with strong organic + referral mix. Not Year-1 plausible. |

**The takeaway**

> *At target acquisition cost, Uncle May's becomes profitable on repeat customer behavior. The work this funding enables is to prove that we can hit those acquisition and retention targets with real Chicago paid spend behind clean measurement.*

The gap between today's $270 CAC and the $40 to $55 target is exactly the work this $25K funds. The two binding constraints (broken Pixel feedback loop, pre-catalog creative) are already addressed in code; what is missing is six months of measured spend behind the fix.

LTV is not yet measured. Buying the time to measure it honestly is a primary use of this funding.

*INFRASTRUCTURE*

## Lean Systems Built Before Raising Capital

Uncle May's is operating today on a complete commerce stack built on operating capital, before any equity raise. The data layer that Chapter 3 eventually monetizes is being built right now, on Chapter 1 cash flow.

This baseline means future operating capital goes toward acquisition and retention proof, not toward platform build.
