## 2. Thesis

**A disciplined grocery investment, not a venture bet.**

## 3. The Broken Black Food Economy

*Three structural pain points the market has left unresolved.*

**Underserved Black consumers.** 67% of Black shoppers visit grocery stores monthly compared with 43% of white shoppers, yet only 35% of Black consumers feel their primary store understands their needs versus 62% of white consumers.<sup>1</sup>

**Invisible Black brands.** 40% of Black consumers say their primary grocery store does not carry culturally relevant foods.<sup>2</sup>

**Excluded Black farmers.** An aggregate demand gap with no organized procurement channel of record. Black farmers represent less than 2% of U.S. agricultural producers.<sup>3</sup>

<sub>1. Deloitte, *State of the Consumer Tracker, Black Consumer Edition*, 2023. 2. NielsenIQ, *Black Consumer Insights*, 2023. 3. USDA Census of Agriculture, 2022.</sub>

## 4. Why Now

- **$340B shift.** Projected growth in Black consumer spending by 2030 (McKinsey Institute for Black Economic Mobility, *The Black Consumer*, 2024).
- **DEI rollback.** Major retailers are publicly pulling back from diversity commitments; Black consumers are 4x more likely to switch purchases to Black-owned alternatives in response (McKinsey, 2024; NPR/Morning Edition reporting on Target boycott, January 2025).
- **No incumbent.** One of the only major cultural demographic groups in the U.S. without a scaled, culturally aligned grocery chain. Every comparable demographic (Hispanic, Indian, Chinese, Korean, Japanese, Caribbean, Italian) has multiple regional or national operators (see slide 8).

## 15. Prepared Foods Strategy

*Prepared foods are doing meaningful economic work in the model. They are also operationally distinct from packaged grocery and warrant explicit underwriting.*

**Menu philosophy.** Culturally rooted ready-to-heat meals built around the existing curated SKU set. Anchor categories: warm-and-serve plates inspired by Black-American culinary tradition (smothered chicken, seasoned greens, mac and cheese, candied yams, baked fish, bean dishes), single-pan family meals, and grab-and-go breakfast and lunch built from on-shelf ingredients.

**Operational model.** In-store kitchen at the flagship leveraging existing refrigeration and prep infrastructure. Phase 2 transition to a regional commissary as the cluster reaches three to five stores, allowing centralized production with daily distribution.

**Margin profile.** Prepared foods carry a meaningfully higher blended gross margin than packaged grocery. The model assumes a conservative ramp from a low single-digit revenue share at open to a mid-teen revenue share by Year 5, contributing to the 70.5% to 64.9% COGS compression.

**Repeat behavior and basket impact.** Prepared foods drive higher visit frequency and larger blended baskets. Customer interviews already show unprompted demand: feedback responses asked for "ready to cook" formats, and the box business currently includes warm-pack chicken thighs as the highest-loved SKU.

## 16. Labor Discipline

*Sophisticated grocery investors diligence labor closely. The flagship is engineered to operate with a meaningfully smaller labor footprint than a conventional supermarket.*

**Smaller-format operating model.** 10,000 sq ft selling area against 30,000+ for a conventional supermarket; ~4,000 SKUs against 30,000 to 50,000. Fewer shelves to face, fewer departments to staff.

**Curated operations.** Purposefully narrow assortment lowers replenishment labor, in-aisle decision time, shrink, and back-of-house complexity.

**Experienced GM already identified.** Matt Weschler, two prior Chicago grocery store starts and exits, currently GM at Wild Onion Grocery. Hits the ground with operating relationships and a known staffing playbook.

**Lean staffing philosophy.** Cross-trained associates rather than narrowly siloed departments. Prepared-foods kitchen staffed at flagship, transitioning to commissary at Phase 2 to consolidate culinary labor across multiple stores.

**Modeled labor share of revenue.** Embedded inside the 19.7% normalized SG&A line in the unit model, consistent with curated and small-format grocery analogs (The Fresh Market, Trader Joe's, Sprouts). Detailed labor schedule available in the data room.

## 25. Investor Alignment Structure

*Clean preferred equity. No tricks.*

- **~$400K minimum preferred equity tranche** corresponding to approximately 16% fully-diluted ownership.
- **8 to 10% accruing preferred return**, non-mandatory cash pay.
- **Distributable cash flow swept to investors** until investors achieve a **cumulative realized cash return of 3.0x to 3.2x invested capital**. Realized cash return is defined as cumulative cash distributions received in respect of the preferred equity, exclusive of unrealized mark-to-market on residual equity.
- **After the threshold is achieved:** sweep terminates, residual equity continues pro rata.

No participating preferred. No ratchets. No conversion gymnastics. Designed for operator-investors who want simple, transparent alignment.

Prove retention, basket size, labor model, margin ramp, and vendor sourcing reliability.

Externally capitalized; pace is a function of capital markets and proven unit economics, not a predetermined fan-out.
