## Part 2: Segment Plan for This Close

This is a $300K tail, not a top-of-funnel raise. The goal is two to four checks of $50K to $100K from operators and family offices who already underwrite SBA-backed real-asset deals. Re-rank the segments accordingly.

### Keep and prioritize (native fit to an SBA-leveraged real asset)
- **Segment F, Black commercial real estate and property investors.** Best fit. They already think in DSCR, NOI, cap rate, and anchor tenant dynamics. Shortest education cycle of any segment.
- **Segment G, Midwest business owners outside food.** Fundamentals evaluators who trust the SBA structure and the acquisition story. Local and fast.
- **Segments A, B, and C, HNW Black angels, former grocery and CPG operators, and impact family offices.** The core operator and family-office target. One correction: our ask is $50K to $100K per check, so drop Segment C's "$200K+ target check" guidance. We are not chasing a single large ticket.

### Use as referral nodes (not front-line)
- **Segment E, wealth advisors to Black HNW clients.** The "one advisor can place several checks" multiplier is real, but advisor due diligence on a single-asset private placement is slow and will not clear inside this window. Use them to source warm introductions, not as a primary cold target.

### Park for a future or larger round
- **Segment D, impact VCs and social-equity funds.** They want priced rounds and ownership, not a $50K to $200K ticket into a $400K round.
- **Segment H, sports, entertainment, and athlete wealth.** High cultural resonance, but business-manager-gatekept and too slow for a 30-day close.
- **Segment I, faith and church leaders.** Longer cycle, frequent accreditation gaps, and the "food access ministry" framing conflicts with our positioning. We are not a food-desert solution, so please avoid that language.
- **Segment J, HBCU endowments and PRI capital.** Longest cycle. Investment-committee governance cannot close a $300K tail by Q3. Hold the "PRI-eligible" claim until we confirm it.

### Geography
Chicago first, then the broader Midwest, for the kept segments. Drop the national casting that segments H, I, and J pulled in.

### Check size
Target $50K to $100K per check. Two to four checks closes the round. We are not chasing a single $200K+ ticket.

## Part 3: Targeting Mechanics

- **Tighten the title filters.** Nearly every segment's title boolean includes President, CEO, Founder, Owner, Director, and VP, so the title clause does almost no filtering. Geography, industry, and keywords are carrying the entire filter. Narrow the titles for precision.
- **Dedupe overlapping segments.** Segments E and H both pull Financial Advisor, RIA, and Wealth Manager titles and will produce duplicate lists. Run them once and dedupe.
- **Favor depth over volume.** About 100 to 200 high-conviction Chicago and Midwest contacts in segments F, G, and A through C will outperform thousands of national contacts for a raise this size.
- **Prioritize warm paths.** Referrals from existing investors and advisors convert far better than cold outreach. Build the introduction lane first, then layer cold on top.
