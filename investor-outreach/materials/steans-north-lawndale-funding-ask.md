## Instrument Recommendation: PRI Line of Credit

**Recommended structure:** Program-Related Investment (PRI) — revolving line of credit

### Why a Line of Credit (Not a Grant)

1. **Aligned with Muldrow's signal.** Mr. Muldrow explicitly referenced a potential line of credit in discussions with Steans, indicating SFF has appetite for this instrument in this context.
2. **Matches Steans' PRI toolkit.** The Steans Family Foundation has a track record of PRIs (below-market loans and lines of credit) for North Lawndale community economic development.
3. **Better for us.** A line of credit provides drawdown flexibility (we only borrow what we need, when we need it) and preserves equity at this stage.
4. **Demonstrates financial discipline.** Entering the conversation with a repayment structure signals we are operators, not grant-seekers — consistent with Anthony's Booth/PE positioning.

### Proposed Terms

| Term | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Instrument | Revolving Line of Credit (PRI) |
| Amount | $125,000 |
| Interest Rate | 0% – 2% (below market; PRI terms) |
| Draw Period | Months 0-6 (as needed) |
| Repayment Begins | Month 7 (post-stabilization) |
| Repayment Term | 24-36 months |
| Collateral | None requested (impact-first PRI) |

### Alternative: Hybrid Structure
If Steans prefers a grant component: **$75,000 grant + $50,000 repayable PRI**. The grant covers equipment and pre-opening costs (one-time, non-recoverable); the PRI covers working capital and inventory (recoverable). This is a fallback — lead with the full $125K line of credit.
