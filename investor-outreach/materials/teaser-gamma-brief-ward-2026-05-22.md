# Uncle May's Produce: A 4th Ward Community Brief

**A Black-owned anchor grocery store for the Hyde Park corridor.**

Bringing a Trader Joe's-scale, curated 10,000 sq ft format to the 4th Ward, sourced through a Black-farmer and Black-CPG supply chain, and built to anchor neighborhood economic development.

> **Opening the Hyde Park flagship June 1, 2026. SBA financing in place. Seeking 4th Ward and Department of Planning partnership to layer in public capital and workforce resources.**

## At A Glance: What the Flagship Delivers to the Ward

| 30-40 | $17-$19/hr | 30+ | $1.5M-$2M |
|---|---|---|---|
| Jobs at the flagship (FT + PT) | Starting wage, above Chicago minimum, FT roles benefits-eligible | Black farmers and Black CPG brands in the active sourcing pipeline | Projected Year-1 procurement flowing back to Black producers |

## Jobs and Local Hiring

**Team composition (Year 1, flagship).** Projected 30 to 40 W-2 positions across produce, butcher, deli, grocery, front-end, and management. Starting hourly wage of $17 to $19, above the Chicago $15.80 minimum, with health benefits available to full-time team members. Department leads and managers paid at competitive Chicago grocery market rates. General Manager Matt Weschler is a two-time grocery operator with active Chicago hiring networks.

**Ward-priority hiring commitment.** Uncle May's will target a majority of its store team hired from the 4th, 5th, and 3rd Wards, with a formal preference for residents of the adjacent census tracts. We will partner with **Chicago Cook Workforce Partnership** for sourcing and screening, **Sunshine Enterprises** for entrepreneur and management pipeline, and the **Chicago Urban League** for workforce inclusion. We are open to a direct referral channel from the 4th Ward office for resident applicants.

## Black Farmer and Black CPG Supply Chain

Uncle May's is the first grocer in Chicago purpose-built to make a Black-owned supply chain operationally viable. The store is the demand engine; the vendor pipeline is the supply engine.

**30+** Black farmers and Black-owned CPG brands in the active sourcing pipeline   ·   **$1.5M to $2M** projected Year-1 procurement flowing back to Black producers   ·   **First-of-its-kind** SKU-level demand data made available to participating suppliers to help them grow

## Corridor and Infrastructure Impact

**Anchor occupancy.** A 10,000 sq ft selling-area grocery store absorbs a high-vacancy commercial footprint and brings a projected **15,000 to 25,000 customer visits per week** to the corridor, with spillover foot traffic to adjacent small businesses.

**Tax and local revenue.** Year-1 revenue of $6.3M generates approximately **$140K per year in Chicago local sales tax**, with property tax uplift from the build-out improvements. Build-out spend of $1.46M is committed to local A&E, GC, and trade work where feasible.

## Public Capital Programs We Are Eligible to Pursue

With 4th Ward support and a Department of Planning introduction, the following programs are credible fits.

| Program | What it does | Why it fits Uncle May's |
|---|---|---|
| **TIF (Tax Increment Financing)** | Reimburses tenant improvement and infrastructure costs in designated districts | $1.46M build-out is the eligible expense base |
| **SBIF (Small Business Improvement Fund)** | DPD grant up to $250K for façade and interior improvements in TIF districts | Direct match to our build-out scope |
| **NOF (Neighborhood Opportunity Fund)** | South and West Side grants, 1 to 3% of CBD development fees, grocery is an eligible use | Hyde Park corridor qualifies; we are a Black-owned anchor grocer |
| **Class 6b / 7a property tax incentives** | Reduced property tax assessments for commercial reinvestment | Aligns with new tenant build-out and job creation |
| **Supplier diversity programs (City, CPS, CHA)** | Vendor pipeline to municipal and quasi-municipal buyers | We are a Black-owned business with verified MBE eligibility |

## What We Are Asking from the 4th Ward

1. **Introduction to the Department of Planning and Development** to review TIF, SBIF, NOF, and grocer-attraction eligibility for the Hyde Park flagship.
2. **Introduction to ward workforce partners** so we can build a direct resident referral channel.
3. **Site package and background on the 47th and Cottage Grove site** for our consideration set for store two.
4. **Letter of support** for any grant or incentive application where the 4th Ward's voice would carry.
5. **Ribbon-cutting visibility** at the June 1 opening so the community sees the project for what it is.
