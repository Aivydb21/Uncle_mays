## Overview

**What is "Blue Sky" compliance?**  
"Blue Sky Laws" are state securities laws that regulate the offer and sale of securities within each state. Even though we are relying on a federal exemption (Rule 506(b)), most states require a **notice filing** when selling securities to residents of that state.

**Rule 506(b) State Filing Requirements:**  
Under Rule 506(b), most states **pre-empt** their registration requirements (thanks to the National Securities Markets Improvement Act of 1996), but they still require:
1. **Notice filing** — Submit Form D (or state equivalent) to the state securities regulator
2. **Filing fee** — Typically $100-$500 per state
3. **Consent to service of process** — Appoint the state securities commissioner as agent for service of legal documents

**Timeline:** Notice filings are typically due **within 15 days** of the first sale to a resident of that state.

## Filing Process (State-by-State)

### Illinois
- **Agency:** Illinois Securities Department
- **Portal:** [https://www.ilsos.gov/departments/securities/home.html](https://www.ilsos.gov/departments/securities/home.html)
- **Filing:** Accept federal Form D (no separate state form)
- **Fee:** $100
- **Timeline:** Within 15 days of first IL sale
- **Consent to Service:** Required (standard form)
- **Annual Renewal:** No
- **Notes:** Home state; expect multiple IL investors

### California
- **Agency:** California Department of Financial Protection and Innovation (DFPI)
- **Portal:** [https://dfpi.ca.gov/](https://dfpi.ca.gov/)
- **Filing:** Form D + Form 25102(f) Notice
- **Fee:** $300 (for offerings >$25K)
- **Timeline:** Within 15 calendar days of first CA sale
- **Consent to Service:** Required (Form U-2)
- **Annual Renewal:** No, but must file amendment for material changes
- **Notes:** Common investor state (LA, SF, SD markets)

### New York
- **Agency:** New York Department of State, Division of Investor Protection and Securities
- **Portal:** [https://www.dos.ny.gov/licensing/securities/](https://www.dos.ny.gov/licensing/securities/)
- **Filing:** Federal Form D accepted
- **Fee:** $300
- **Timeline:** Within 15 days of first NY sale
- **Consent to Service:** Required (Consent to Service of Process form)
- **Annual Renewal:** No
- **Notes:** NYC venture capital hub; expect NY investors

### Texas
- **Agency:** Texas State Securities Board
- **Portal:** [https://www.ssb.texas.gov/](https://www.ssb.texas.gov/)
- **Filing:** Form D via EDGAR (federal) accepted
- **Fee:** $195
- **Timeline:** Within 15 days of first TX sale
- **Consent to Service:** Required
- **Annual Renewal:** No
- **Notes:** Large investor base; Houston, Dallas, Austin markets

### Florida
- **Agency:** Florida Office of Financial Regulation
- **Portal:** [https://flofr.gov/](https://flofr.gov/)
- **Filing:** Form D via EDGAR accepted + state notice
- **Fee:** $500
- **Timeline:** Within 15 days of first FL sale
- **Consent to Service:** Required
- **Annual Renewal:** No
- **Notes:** Miami, Tampa investor markets

### Georgia
- **Agency:** Georgia Secretary of State, Securities and Business Regulation Division
- **Portal:** [https://sos.ga.gov/securities](https://sos.ga.gov/securities)
- **Filing:** Form D via EDGAR accepted
- **Fee:** $250
- **Timeline:** Within 15 days of first GA sale
- **Consent to Service:** Required
- **Annual Renewal:** No
- **Notes:** Atlanta market; expect some GA investors

## Filing Workflow

### Step 1: Identify Investor State (Day 0)
- When investor executes SAFE and provides residential address, log their state in the tracker above
- If this is the **first investor from that state**, mark the "First Sale Date"

### Step 2: Calculate Filing Deadline (Day 0)
- Most states: **15 days** from first sale to a resident of that state
- Mark the filing deadline in the tracker

### Step 3: Prepare State Filing Package (Days 1-5)
- **Federal Form D:** Use the Form D filed with SEC (same document)
- **Consent to Service of Process:** Download state-specific form from state securities website
- **Payment:** Prepare check or credit card payment for filing fee

### Step 4: Submit State Notice (Days 5-10)
- File online (if state has electronic portal) or mail to state securities agency
- Include:
  - Copy of federal Form D (filed via SEC EDGAR)
  - Consent to Service of Process (signed by CEO or authorized officer)
  - Filing fee (check or credit card)
  - Cover letter identifying the offering and federal exemption claimed

### Step 5: Track Confirmation (Days 10-30)
- Most states issue a confirmation number or receipt within 2-4 weeks
- Log the confirmation number in the tracker
- Retain copy of filing receipt in corporate records (`investor-outreach/diligence/state-filings/[STATE]/`)

### Step 6: Monitor for Amendments
- If we file an **amendment to federal Form D** (e.g., material change, annual amendment), check if states require amended notices
- Most states do NOT require amendments unless there is a material change to offering terms

## Common State Filing Mistakes (Avoid These)

1. **Missing the 15-day deadline** — Late filings can result in penalties ($500-$5,000 per state) and loss of exemption. Set calendar reminders.
2. **Not filing in investor's state** — Always file based on investor's **residential address** (not business address, even if they're investing via an entity).
3. **Forgetting Consent to Service of Process** — Most states require a signed consent form. Don't skip this.
4. **Using wrong filing fee** — Fees vary by state and offering size. Check the state fee schedule before submitting payment.
5. **Not tracking multi-state investors** — If an investor has residences in multiple states (e.g., NY + FL), file in ALL states where they maintain a residence.
