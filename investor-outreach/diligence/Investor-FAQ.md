## Deal Structure

### Q: What are the terms of this SAFE?
**A:** We are raising via YC Post-Money SAFE with the following terms:
- **Post-Money Valuation Cap:** $5,000,000
- **Discount Rate:** 20%
- **Minimum Investment:** $25,000 per investor
- **Target Raise:** $400K minimum (triggers SBA facility) to $750K maximum
- **Exemption:** SEC Rule 506(b) — accredited investors only, no general solicitation

### Q: How does the $5M cap work?
**A:** The post-money valuation cap sets a maximum price for your SAFE conversion. When we raise a priced equity round (Series Seed or Series A), your SAFE converts to equity at the lower of:
1. The **Safe Price** (calculated using the $5M cap), or
2. The **Discount Price** (20% discount to the new round price)

**Example:** If we raise a Series A at a $20M post-money valuation, your SAFE converts at a $5M cap (effectively a 75% discount to the Series A price). If we raise at a $4M valuation, you convert at the 20% discount price.

- **Capital leverage:** Your $400K minimum investment activates $2M+ in senior debt, creating 5:1 capital leverage
- **De-risked execution:** Site identified (Hyde Park), GM hired (Matt Weschler, 2 prior grocery exits), architect and designer selected (Civic Projects, Food Market Designs)
- **Strategic positioning:** First-mover in data-driven Black grocery retail; $100B+ TAM

The 20% discount is standard for early-stage SAFEs with a valuation cap.

## Business Model & Market

### Q: What is Uncle May's business model?
**A:** Uncle May's is building the first **data and distribution platform for Black food consumption**. We operate neighborhood-format grocery stores (7,500–12,000 sq ft) targeting historically Black American communities with higher willingness to pay.

**Revenue model:**
- **Retail Layer:** Grocery sales, private label (20-30% gross margin)
- **Vendor Layer:** Distribution margin, placement fees (30-45% gross margin)
- **Data Layer:** Market insights, demand analytics (60-70% gross margin)
- **Platform Layer:** Vendor services, marketplace (70-80% gross margin)

The flagship store is the proof-of-concept. Once we demonstrate $6.3M Year 1 revenue and 15.3% EBITDA margin, we scale to 82 stores nationally over 10 years ($625M revenue by Year 10).

### Q: Why focus on Black consumers specifically?
**A:** Three reasons:
1. **Underserved market:** $100B+ annual Black grocery spend, but no national grocery chain specifically targeting Black cultural preferences (produce, proteins, prepared foods)
2. **Proven demand:** 97% intent-to-shop from 100+ surveyed Hyde Park consumers; 89.6% would refer to friends/family
3. **Cultural capital:** Uncle May's is founded by Anthony Ivy (Black, Chicago Booth MBA, family heritage in Southern farming) and advised by industry leaders Zoe Rowell (COO, 15+ yrs Amazon Retail), Jua Mitchell (CFO, 20+ yrs M&A/IB), Tara Weymon (CMO, Global Marketing Unilever/P&G)

We are NOT a food desert solution. We target affluent Black communities with higher economic capacity and cultural pride.

### Q: What is your competitive moat?
**A:** Four defensible advantages:
1. **Cultural authenticity:** Black founder, Black leadership, deep understanding of Black food preferences and shopping behaviors
2. **Data ownership:** We capture demand data at the point of purchase, creating proprietary insights into the $100B Black grocery market
3. **First-mover advantage:** No national competitor specifically targeting Black consumers with a grocery + data platform model
4. **Distribution leverage:** As we scale, we become the preferred distribution channel for vendors seeking access to Black consumers

Comparable: Cardenas Markets (Hispanic-focused grocery, $1B KKR buyout), H Mart (Asian-focused, $1B+ valuation).

### Q: What is your exit strategy?
**A:** Three paths:
1. **Strategic acquisition** (most likely) — Acquired by Kroger, Albertsons, Whole Foods, or private equity (KKR, Bain Capital) seeking access to Black consumer data and distribution. Comparable: Cardenas Markets ($1B KKR acquisition).
2. **IPO** (aspirational) — Go public after achieving $100M+ revenue and demonstrating unit economics at scale.
3. **Recapitalization** — Take distributions once stores are cash-flow positive and use operating cash flow to buy out early investors.

**Target timeline:** Exit in 7-10 years after achieving national scale (30-50 stores) and demonstrating strong unit economics.

## Legal & Compliance

### Q: What securities exemption are you using?
**A:** We are relying on **SEC Rule 506(b)** under Regulation D, which allows us to raise unlimited capital from accredited investors without general solicitation. This means:
- All investors must be **accredited investors** (income >$200K/year or net worth >$1M excluding primary residence)
- We can only solicit investors with whom we have a pre-existing, substantive relationship
- Investors will self-certify their accredited status (no independent verification required)

### Q: Will you file a Form D with the SEC?
**A:** Yes. We will file **Form D** with the SEC within 15 days after the first SAFE is executed and payment is received. Form D is a notice filing (not a registration) and is publicly available on the SEC's EDGAR database.

### Q: Do I need to provide financial documentation to prove I'm accredited?
**A:** No. Under Rule 506(b), you will self-certify your accredited investor status by checking the applicable box on the SAFE signature page (Schedule A: Accredited Investor Verification). We will rely on your representation.

If we switch to **Rule 506(c)**, we would be required to independently verify your accredited status (tax returns, bank statements, CPA letter). We are currently using 506(b).

### Q: What are the transfer restrictions on the SAFE?
**A:** SAFEs are **restricted securities** and cannot be freely traded or transferred. You must hold the SAFE until it converts to equity (at a future priced round) or until the company has an exit event. Any transfer requires company consent and compliance with securities laws.

### Q: What reporting will I receive as a SAFE holder?
**A:** Uncle May's will provide:
- **Quarterly updates** (business progress, financials, key milestones) via email
- **Annual financial statements** (once flagship is operational)
- **Material event notifications** (new funding rounds, acquisitions, IPO)
- **Conversion notice** (when your SAFE converts to equity)

You will NOT have board seats, voting rights, or information rights until your SAFE converts to equity (at which point you will have pro-rata shareholder rights).
