**Date:** 2026-05-08
**Author:** Anthony Ivy + Claude
**Audience for this brief:** internal, used to align outreach copy and target list

## The shift in one sentence

Uncle May's is not a venture-stage consumer brand. It is a **self-funded searcher deal** structured as a single-asset, SBA-leveraged, preferred-equity development deal with an experienced GM and a defined cash sweep. The audience that underwrites this deal pattern is **self-funded searcher LPs and the family offices that back them**, not traditional VCs.

## Why the prior targeting missed

The prior outreach (Tier 1 thesis-aligned investors, Tier 2 warm investors) was built for a VC pattern-match: founder, market thesis, scale path, platform optionality. The Uncle May's structure does not fit that pattern. Specifically:

- The base case is a **single-asset 18.4 percent five-year IRR / 23.3 percent ten-year IRR** at Hyde Park. VCs do not underwrite single-asset cash flow at those return targets.
- Senior debt is a **$2.0M SBA 7(a) facility** with conditional approval. VCs do not finance equity stacks underneath SBA loans.
- The equity ask is **$400K preferred** with an 8 to 10 percent accruing return and a 3.0 to 3.2x cumulative realized cash return sweep. That is a self-funded searcher cap-table pattern, not a SAFE or priced equity round.
- Anthony is the **operator-CEO**, not a founder building toward a Series A. Matt Weschler is the named GM with two prior Chicago grocery exits.
- The platform / data thesis is **explicitly secondary**, unlocked only after Phase 1 unit economics validate.

These are the exact attributes that self-funded searcher LPs and ETA-aware family offices look for. They are also the attributes that VC reviewers screen out at the first pass.

## Who the right audience is

**Tier 1 priority: ETA / self-funded searcher LPs**

LPs who already write checks alongside individual operators using SBA 7(a) plus preferred equity. They have seen this deal pattern dozens of times. They underwrite the operator, the asset, and the capital structure. Examples of the pattern they back: a Booth or HBS MBA buys a sub-$10M EBITDA business with SBA leverage, raises $300K to $1M of equity from LPs at a clean preferred, runs the business for 5 to 10 years.

The Uncle May's variant is a development deal (new-build flagship at an existing $8M+ revenue location) rather than an acquisition, but the capital structure and the operator-LP relationship are identical to a typical self-funded SF deal.

**Tier 2 priority: family offices that back self-funded searchers**

Family offices, often with operator-investor principals, that allocate to self-funded searcher deals as part of a small-cap private credit or operating company sleeve. They typically write $50K to $250K checks per deal. They appreciate mission-aligned and impact angles when present, but they underwrite cash flow first.

**Out of scope, deliberately**

- Traditional two-step search funds (with stipend, then acquisition, then 25 percent sponsor carry). Anthony is not raising a search fund.
- Independent sponsors looking to lead the deal. Anthony is the operator, not seeking a sponsor on top.
- Pure venture capital. Wrong return profile, wrong stage, wrong structure.
- DTC / consumer growth equity. Wrong asset class.

## What this audience cares about

In rough order of weight in their underwriting model:

1. **The operator.** Why is Anthony Ivy right for this asset, this market, and this category? Background, relevant operating exposure, why grocery and why now.
2. **The asset.** Hyde Park, $8M+ existing revenue at this address for three years, 10,000 sq ft, transit-connected, $100K UChicago tenant improvement commitment.
3. **The capital structure.** $2.0M SBA 7(a) senior, $400K preferred equity, $100K TI. Senior debt covenant headroom (DSCR 4.9x to 4.0x stabilized).
4. **The operator team.** Matt Weschler as named GM, two prior Chicago grocery exits. Experienced grocery operator, not a first-time GM hire.
5. **Downside.** What does the asset do at 75 percent and 50 percent of plan. Refinance optionality. Personal guarantee posture (SBA-typical).
6. **Discipline.** Single-store proof before any expansion. No forced growth. Platform / data is optional upside, not the basis of the underwriting.
