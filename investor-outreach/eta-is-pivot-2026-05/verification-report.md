## 3. Black ETA Network Findings

There is no single organized Black-led ETA Slack/Discord with active LP-introducer activity that gates capital for self-funded searchers. What does exist, in descending order of relevance:

- New Majority Capital (newmajoritycapital.com) and its NMC Foundation (nmcfound.org). Runs accelerator programming for underrepresented searchers, partners with ICIC on the bETA program, and is the most institutionally serious Black/BIPOC-tilted ETA ecosystem builder. Worth a direct outreach as introducer.
- ICIC BIPOC ETA program (icic.org/bipoc-entrepreneurship-through-acquisition). Eight-week program, Atlanta and Rhode Island cohorts. Education-first, not LP-first, but the alumni list is real.
- ETA Capital Fund (etafund1.com), formerly Minority Business Accelerator Fund, Cincinnati. $26M Midwest-focused vehicle, six-hour radius from Cincinnati that explicitly includes Illinois. Check sizes $1M to $2.5M, equity/debt/hybrid, with 50% acquisition allocation. This is the closest thing to a Black-tilted LP that could actually write into Uncle May's. Strong fit on geography (Chicago is in their radius) and on acquisition stage. Highest-priority addition for Anthony.
- Booth African American MBA Association (boothaambaa.com) and Booth ETA Group (groups.chicagobooth.edu/etag). Anthony's Booth alumni status is the natural intro vector here. Not LPs themselves but a high-trust introducer network.
- HBS African-American Alumni Association (hbsaaa.org). Real, 3,100 members, but ETA is an event topic (Prof. Archie Jones Jr. talks) rather than a formal LP subgroup. Useful as warm intro lookup if Anthony has any HBS-side personal connection.
- Adjacencies that were considered and downgraded: BLCK VC (venture capital, not ETA), The Bridges Group (multiple unrelated funds with similar names, none are Black ETA LPs), Black Entrepreneurs Forum (general entrepreneurship, no ETA flavor), ManyManyMillions (Twitter/community, not an LP channel), MEOA (Minority Equality Opportunities Acquisition Inc., a SPAC for Black-owned firms; structurally interesting but not a self-funded-search LP).

Honest summary: Anthony should not promise the deck a "Black ETA network" is being engaged. What he can credibly say is that ETA Capital Fund (Cincinnati), New Majority Capital, Booth AAMBA, and HBSAAA are being engaged as introducers and, in ETA Capital Fund's case, as a possible direct investor. That is real and defensible.
