## Why Uncle May's is a Documentary Subject

The story has the three things doc producers look for:
1. **Stakes** — Black farmers (less than 2% of U.S. farms) and Black households ($100B in spend, no one building for them) are both at structural disadvantage. Uncle May's connects them.
2. **Character** — Anthony Ivy (Booth MBA, PE/M&A, awards, SXSW) is a credible, articulate founder with a clear arc.
3. **Visual access** — produce-box pack-out, farm visits, Hyde Park flagship build-out, store opening, customer stories. Built-in narrative beats.

## The Pitch (Tailorable per Producer)

**Subject line:** Black founder building distribution from Black farmers to Black households, on-the-ground access available

**One paragraph:** Uncle May's Produce is building the distribution layer for Black food. We ship produce boxes from Black farmers to Black households today and open a 10,000 sq ft Hyde Park grocery flagship in 2026. The two-sided story (Black farmers as supply, Black households as demand) is rarely told together. We're offering selective access — farm visits, the build-out of the flagship, our customers and farmer partners — to a producer or team who wants to capture the story as it happens. Awards: 2025 TechRise, World Business Chicago Food Innovation Award. Speaking SXSW 2026 on food systems. Founder is a Booth MBA with a PE background. Open to a 15-minute intro call.

## Decision Tree

- **If a producer replies with interest:** Schedule 15-min intro. Do NOT commit to exclusivity in first call. Offer a follow-up site visit.
- **If 3 producers pass:** Refine the pitch (different angle: farmer-led, founder-led, customer-led).
- **If no producer bites by Day 90:** Document learnings. Decide whether to keep pursuing in next sprint, shelve, or try the build-it-ourselves route (creator partnership scaling, sizzle reel produced via collab budget).
