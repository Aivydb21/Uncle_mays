## What Makes Uncle May's Different

### The Thesis
Black households are underserved by grocery retail not because of location, but because no one has built the distribution infrastructure or the data platform to understand their preferences and meet them at scale. Black farmers face the inverse problem: real product, no reliable channel to the consumers who want it most. Uncle May's solves both.

### The Model
- **Today:** Produce boxes sourced from Black farmers, delivered to Black households. Validates demand, builds the customer base, opens the data flywheel before the doors of the flagship open.
- **Next:** 10,000 sq ft Hyde Park flagship store, neighborhood format optimized for replication.
- **The platform:** Every box, every basket, every receipt feeds a first-of-its-kind data set on Black food consumption at scale.
- **Institutional-quality unit economics:** 35% gross margins, 15.3% EBITDA margins, 33% 5-year IRR on the flagship model.

### The Market
- **TAM:** $100B+ annual Black grocery spend
- **SAM:** $33.8B (Black Families plus Black Working Professionals)
- **Beachhead:** Black women, ages 25 to 35
- **Proven demand:** 97% intent-to-shop from 100+ surveyed consumers, 89.6% would refer

## Why This Matters Now

1. **The conversation about Black food access is broken:** Most narratives miss that Black households are underserved on the demand side and Black farmers are underserved on the supply side. Uncle May's connects both.
2. **$100B+ TAM has been ignored:** Traditional grocers have not built for Black consumers as a primary audience. Uncle May's is.
3. **Black farmers need a channel:** Black-owned farms make up less than 2% of U.S. farms and most lack reliable distribution to the households that want to buy from them. The produce box closes that loop.
4. **Institutional capital is available:** SBA conditional approval proves the model is bankable.
5. **Chicago is the proving ground:** Diverse neighborhoods, strong Black consumer base, robust food culture.
6. **Data is the moat:** First-mover advantage in understanding Black food consumption at scale.
