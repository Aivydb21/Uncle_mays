Per the May 11 request, this is the line-item budget for the $50,000 top grant, anchored to the four allocation categories in the original deck. Each line funds work that the surrounding $2.5M capital stack (SBA 7(a) loan, UChicago tenant-improvement support, and the SAFE round) is structurally unable to cover: small-farm pre-payment, brand-partner shelf activation in lieu of slotting fees, community-scale launch programming, and verified impact reporting. The $10,000 secondary-grant scenario follows the main table.

---

## Allocation 1 — Black-Farmer Supplier Onboarding — $15,000

*Outcome: 12-20 Black farms delivering product at flagship open.*

| Sub-item | Amount | Note |
|---|---|---|
| GAP and food-safety certification support | $5,000 | $300-500 per farm × 12-15 farms; SBA capital does not fund vendor-side certification. |
| First-PO funding (upfront vs. net-30/60) | $5,000 | Removes the working-capital barrier small Black farms face. SBA covenants prohibit using SBA proceeds for this. |
| Cold-chain logistics setup | $3,000 | Refrigerated transport, food-grade packaging, pallets and crates for the new supplier network. |
| Vendor onboarding admin | $2,000 | Agreements, certificates of insurance, ACH setup, sample buys across the cohort. |

## Allocation 2 — Black CPG Brand Shelf-Activation — $15,000

*Outcome: 40-60 Black-owned brands on flagship shelves at open.*

| Sub-item | Amount | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Private-label packaging design and small-run print | $5,000 | ~$80-100 per SKU across ~50 SKUs; replaces conventional slotting fees small brands cannot pay. |
| First-PO inventory fulfillment | $6,000 | Initial inventory across 40-60 brand partners; distinct from the SBA-funded mainline inventory budget. |
| In-store merchandising and signage | $2,500 | Shelf-talkers, brand-story signage, dedicated Black-CPG section identity. Not in build-out scope. |
| Brand marketing co-op | $1,500 | Joint social and email features for partners, in lieu of slotting fees. |

## Allocation 3 — Community-Launch Outreach — $10,000

*Outcome: Flagship opens as a community event, not a soft launch.*

| Sub-item | Amount | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Grand-opening community event | $4,000 | Venue, catering by Black-owned partners, signage, sound, family programming. |
| Neighborhood hiring fair | $2,500 | Recruiting 25-35 hires from the South Side: venue, recruiter time, materials, finalist stipends. |
| Farmer and brand-partner showcase week | $2,000 | In-store demos and tastings during the first seven days; supplier network as the headline. |
| Earned-media outreach and opening-day photography | $1,500 | Local press outreach, opening-day photography for the FSFI six-month report. |

## Allocation 4 — Six-Month Impact Measurement and Reporting — $10,000

*Outcome: Verified, FSFI-reportable impact numbers at month six. Uncle May's existing analytics stack (POS, GA4, Stripe, BigQuery, dbt) supplies the data; this allocation funds the human work.*

| Sub-item | Amount | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Community impact survey | $3,500 | Customer survey: store-visit reduction (2-6 stores to 1), cultural-relevance score, neighborhood NPS, dollar-store recapture. |
| Farmer and brand-partner testimonial collection | $2,500 | Video and written testimonials for the six-month report and FSFI's own impact storytelling. |
| Six-month FSFI follow-up report production | $2,500 | Writing, data compilation, and design of the report committed in the original application. |
| FSFI-specific KPI dashboard | $1,500 | Live dashboard on existing analytics infrastructure, dedicated to the six FSFI-reportable dimensions. |
