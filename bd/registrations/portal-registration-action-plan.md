## Portal 1: City of Chicago iSupplier

**Priority:** HIGH  
**Estimated Time:** 30-45 minutes  
**Processing Time:** 3 business days  

### Step-by-Step Registration

1. **Determine Registration Path**
   - **IF** Uncle May's has NEVER had a contract with City of Chicago AND has NEVER received a payment from City of Chicago:
     - Use **New Vendor Registration** path
     - Go to: https://www.chicago.gov/city/en/depts/dps/isupplier/login-register.html
     - Click "New Vendor Registration" button
   - **IF** Uncle May's has previously registered, had a contract, or received a payment:
     - Send email to: CustomerSupport@cityofchicago.org
     - Subject line: "Request an iSupplier Invitation"
     - Include: Company name, EIN, contact info

2. **Complete New Vendor Registration Form** (if applicable)
   - Company legal name: Uncle May's Produce
   - Federal EIN
   - Business address
   - Primary contact name, title, email, phone
   - Banking information for ACH payments
   - Upload IRS W-9 form
   - Select business categories/commodity codes (search for: fresh produce, food service, grocery, wholesale food)

3. **Wait for Approval**
   - Processing time: 3 business days
   - You'll receive email confirmation when approved

4. **Set Up iSupplier Portal Access**
   - Once approved, log in to: https://eprocurement.cityofchicago.org/
   - Complete profile
   - Set up bid alert preferences for food service categories
   - Verify company information is correct

5. **Sign Up for Bid Alerts**
   - Go to: https://www.chicago.gov/city/en/sites/procurementinformation/home.html
   - Subscribe to email notifications for:
     - Food & Beverage
     - Fresh Produce
     - Food Service
     - Grocery/Retail

### Success Criteria
- [ ] iSupplier vendor account active and approved
- [ ] Can log in to portal successfully
- [ ] Bid alerts configured for food service categories
- [ ] Profile complete with W-9 on file

## Portal 2: Cook County Bonfire

**Priority:** HIGH  
**Estimated Time:** 30-45 minutes  
**Processing Time:** Immediate (account active upon registration)

### Step-by-Step Registration

1. **Create Bonfire Account**
   - Go to: https://cookcountyil.bonfirehub.com/portal/
   - Click "Register" or "Create Account"
   - Enter email address and create password
   - Verify email address

2. **Complete Vendor Profile**
   - Company legal name: Uncle May's Produce
   - Federal EIN
   - Business address
   - Primary contact name, title, email, phone
   - DUNS number (if available) or SAM.gov UEI
   - Business classification (check: Minority-owned if applicable, Small Business)
   - Upload certifications (if any): MBE, WBE, etc. (we don't have these yet)

3. **Select UNSPSC Commodity Codes**
   - Visit: https://www.unspsc.org/ to search for relevant codes
   - Select ALL codes that apply to Uncle May's produce business:
     - 50000000: Food Beverage and Tobacco Products
     - 50300000: Fruit and vegetable products
     - 50400000: Dairy products and eggs
     - 90100000: Food and Beverage Services (catering, food service)
     - Search for: "fresh produce", "grocery", "food distribution", "wholesale food"
   - **IMPORTANT:** Select generously — bid alerts are based on these codes

4. **Enable Automatic Notifications**
   - In profile settings, turn ON email notifications
   - Notifications will be sent when opportunities match your selected commodity codes
   - Set notification frequency: Daily or as posted (recommended: as posted)

5. **Explore Current Opportunities**
   - Click "Open Opportunities" tab
   - Filter by commodity codes related to food service
   - Bookmark for weekly monitoring

### Success Criteria
- [ ] Bonfire vendor account created and active
- [ ] Profile complete with commodity codes selected
- [ ] Email notifications enabled
- [ ] Can view current Cook County contract opportunities

## Portal 3: Illinois Procurement Gateway (IPG)

**Priority:** HIGH  
**Estimated Time:** 45-60 minutes (requires additional IL registrations first)  
**Processing Time:** Varies (Chief Procurement Office review required)

### PRE-REGISTRATION REQUIREMENTS

**BEFORE** you can register on IPG, you MUST obtain these Illinois-specific registration numbers:

#### 3A. Illinois Secretary of State — Business Registration
- **Portal:** https://www.ilsos.gov/corporatellc/
- **Purpose:** Register to do business in Illinois
- **Required For:** IPG registration
- **Action:** 
  - Check if Uncle May's Produce is already registered as an Illinois business entity
  - If NOT registered: File Articles of Incorporation or Foreign Registration
  - Obtain Certificate of Good Standing

#### 3B. Illinois State Board of Elections — Registration
- **Portal:** https://www.elections.il.gov/
- **Purpose:** Political contribution disclosure requirement for state contractors
- **Required For:** IPG registration
- **Action:**
  - Search for Uncle May's Produce in the State Board of Elections database
  - If NOT registered: Complete the State Board of Elections Registration
  - Obtain State Board of Elections Registration Number

#### 3C. Illinois Department of Human Rights (IDHR) — Public Contract Number
- **Portal:** https://www2.illinois.gov/dhr/Pages/default.aspx
- **Required ONLY IF:** Uncle May's has 15 or more employees
- **Purpose:** Equal employment opportunity compliance for state contractors
- **Action:**
  - Determine if Uncle May's currently has 15+ employees (include FTE + part-time)
  - If YES: Register with IDHR and obtain Public Contract Number
  - If NO: Skip this requirement (note on IPG application)

### Step-by-Step IPG Registration (AFTER pre-reqs completed)

1. **Create IPG Account**
   - Go to: https://ipg.vendorreg.com/
   - Click "Register" or "New Vendor"
   - Enter email and create password

2. **Complete Vendor Registration Application**
   - Company legal name: Uncle May's Produce
   - Federal EIN
   - Business address
   - Illinois Secretary of State Registration Number
   - Illinois State Board of Elections Registration Number
   - IDHR Public Contract Number (if 15+ employees, otherwise select "Not Applicable")
   - SAM.gov UEI (from federal registration)
   - Primary contact info

3. **Upload Required Documents**
   - IRS W-9
   - Certificate of Good Standing (from IL Secretary of State)
   - Proof of IL business registration
   - Any certifications (MBE, WBE if applicable — we don't have these yet)

4. **Select Business Codes**
   - Choose categories that match Uncle May's services:
     - Food & Beverage
     - Fresh Produce
     - Food Distribution
     - Wholesale Grocery
     - Food Service

5. **Submit Application**
   - Review all information for accuracy
   - Submit to Chief Procurement Office for review
   - Wait for approval and IPG registration number

6. **Set Up Illinois Procurement Bulletin Account** (for e-Bidding)
   - Go to: https://www.illinois.gov/cms/procurement/
   - Register for Procurement Bulletin email notifications
   - Use your IPG registration number to enable e-Bidding capability

### Success Criteria
- [ ] All pre-registration requirements completed (IL Secretary of State, State Board of Elections, IDHR if applicable)
- [ ] IPG vendor registration submitted
- [ ] IPG registration number received and in good standing
- [ ] Illinois Procurement Bulletin account created for e-Bidding
- [ ] Email notifications enabled for food service categories

## Portal 4: Chicago Public Schools (CPS)

**Priority:** MEDIUM  
**Estimated Time:** Monitoring/relationship-based (not direct registration)  
**Processing Time:** N/A (must win solicitation or obtain school sponsorship)

### CPS Vendor Strategy (NON-TRADITIONAL PATH)

**IMPORTANT:** CPS does NOT accept unsolicited vendor applications. There are only TWO paths to becoming a CPS vendor:

#### Path A: Win a Public Solicitation
1. **Monitor Open Solicitations**
   - Go to: https://www.cps.edu/procurement/contracting-opportunities/
   - Check weekly for RFPs/RFQs related to:
     - Food service
     - Fresh produce
     - School nutrition programs
     - Cafeteria supply
   - Bookmark: https://www.demandstar.com/app/agencies/illinois/chicago-public-schools/ (third-party RFP aggregator)

2. **Respond to Relevant RFPs**
   - When a food service RFP is posted, prepare a competitive bid
   - Follow RFP instructions exactly
   - Include: pricing, product list, delivery capabilities, food safety certifications
   - **COORDINATE WITH COO** before bidding to confirm supply capacity

3. **If Awarded Contract**
   - CPS will onboard Uncle May's as vendor
   - Register on CPS Supplier Portal for electronic POs
   - Annual spend limit: $35K unless Board contract is executed

#### Path B: Obtain School Sponsorship
1. **Identify Hyde Park/Kenwood CPS Schools**
   - Target schools near flagship location:
     - Kenwood Academy High School (5015 S Blackstone Ave)
     - Ray Elementary School (5631 S Kimbark Ave)
     - Bret Harte Elementary (1556 E 56th St)
     - Others in Hyde Park area

2. **Build Relationship with Principal/Food Service Director**
   - Approach: "We're a new Black-owned produce company opening a store in Hyde Park, and we'd love to supply your school with fresh, high-quality produce"
   - Offer: Taste samples, school tour of our operation, competitive pricing
   - Goal: Get Principal or Network Chief to email purchasing@cps.edu requesting vendor sponsorship

3. **Principal Initiates Sponsorship Request**
   - Principal/Network Chief/Department Director sends email to: purchasing@cps.edu
   - Subject: "Vendor Sponsorship Application Request"
   - Content: Intent to use Uncle May's Produce for [specific use case]
   - **CRITICAL:** This request MUST come from CPS, not from Uncle May's

4. **CPS Procurement Reviews**
   - If approved, CPS will send vendor application
   - Complete application and submit
   - Once approved, register on CPS Supplier Portal

### CPS Vendor Monitoring Strategy

**Weekly Monitoring:**
- Check https://www.cps.edu/procurement/contracting-opportunities/ every Monday
- Set up Google Alert: "Chicago Public Schools food service RFP"
- Subscribe to CPS Procurement email list if available

**Relationship Building:**
- Add Hyde Park CPS schools to institutional outreach target list
- Coordinate with Sales/Marketing on school engagement strategy
- Consider: School nutrition director outreach, PTA/parent group outreach, community engagement events

### Success Criteria
- [ ] Weekly RFP monitoring process established
- [ ] Hyde Park CPS schools identified for sponsorship path
- [ ] Relationships initiated with 2-3 school principals or food service directors
- [ ] Responded to at least 1 relevant RFP (if posted)
