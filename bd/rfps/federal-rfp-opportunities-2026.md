## How to Find More Federal Opportunities

### SAM.gov Search Strategy

1. **Go to [SAM.gov Contract Opportunities](https://sam.gov/opportunities)**
2. **Search by keywords:**
   - "fresh produce"
   - "fruits and vegetables"
   - "food distribution"
   - "subsistence"
   - "commissary"
   - "USDA school lunch"
3. **Filter by Set-Aside:**
   - Select "Small Business Set-Aside" or "Total Small Business" (Uncle May's qualifies)
4. **Filter by NAICS Code:**
   - 424480 — Fresh Fruit and Vegetable Merchant Wholesalers
   - 424490 — Other Grocery and Related Products Merchant Wholesalers
   - 445230 — Fruit and Vegetable Markets
5. **Filter by Agency:**
   - Department of Defense (DLA Troop Support)
   - Department of Agriculture (USDA AMS)
   - Department of Veterans Affairs (VA)
   - Health and Human Services (HHS)
6. **Set up email alerts:**
   - Save search criteria
   - Receive daily/weekly notifications for new matching opportunities

## Risk Assessment

### Risks to Federal Contracting Strategy

**Risk 1: Volume Capacity**
- Federal contracts = large volume commitments ($500K-$5M annually)
- Uncle May's supply chain may not be ready for this scale pre-flagship opening
- **Mitigation:** Start with smaller contracts (VA hospitals, USDA AMS items) before pursuing $300M+ DLA contracts

**Risk 2: Logistics Costs**
- Out-of-state contracts (TX, NV, AZ) require regional delivery infrastructure
- Shipping from Chicago may not be cost-competitive
- **Mitigation:** Partner with regional distributors OR focus on Midwest contracts (Iowa, VA Chicago)

**Risk 3: Certification Requirements**
- GAP/HACCP certifications may be required (cost + time investment)
- If Uncle May's doesn't have these, bid timeline extends 90-180 days
- **Mitigation:** Verify requirements per RFP before bidding; prioritize contracts that don't require expensive certs

**Risk 4: Bid Competition**
- Federal contracts attract large, established produce distributors
- Uncle May's = small, new entrant without federal contracting track record
- **Mitigation:** Focus on small business set-asides; leverage mission-driven brand story; start with smaller contracts to build track record

**Risk 5: Long Sales Cycles**
- Federal contracting = 6-12 months from RFP to contract award
- May not generate revenue in time for flagship opening (2026)
- **Mitigation:** Start bidding process now; expect 2027 revenue from federal contracts, not 2026
