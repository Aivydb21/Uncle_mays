# Executive Summary - Uncle May's Strategy Review (2026-06-07)

Uncle May's is a viable food-DTC brand crippled by a single root cause: a 43-SKU produce-only catalog that makes basket completion impossible, which is why paid conversion sits at roughly 0.46% (4-8x below the food-DTC benchmark) and every paid dollar is wasted. The move is a "catalog-first, then funnel" sequence: launch Wave A (soul-food basket) and Wave B (commodity staples) in parallel to reach 80-100 live SKUs, resolve the EXP-002 daily-delivery SLA, activate the Black-owned badge, and hold all paid media, the CRO operator hire, and any platform migration behind a single three-metric relaunch gate. The base-case business case is strongly positive (an assumption-labeled $43K-$72K inventory investment recouped in roughly 8-14 weeks if CVR moves from 0.46% toward a conservative 1.5%), and the dominant risk is the assumption that assortment, not funnel friction, is the primary conversion driver, which must be tested with LogRocket session recordings before paid spend resumes. The ask is to approve catalog expansion as the sole capital priority, ratify the relaunch gate as the governance trigger for all deferred spend, and re-subscribe LogRocket so the decision is measurable.
