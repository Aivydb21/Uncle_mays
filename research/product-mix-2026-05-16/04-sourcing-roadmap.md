## TL;DR

The Suppliers DB has solid breadth (1,320 records, 826 with email) but **shallow Midwest depth**. Most Black-owned suppliers in the DB are scattered nationally (LA, TN, VA, NC, MS, TX, MD, KY). Chicago / IL / IN / MI / WI is the home market and the only one with realistic same-day or next-day fulfillment without freight aggregation.

Regional supplier counts (with email, IL/IN/MI/WI):
- Produce: 21
- Meat & Seafood: 4
- Pantry / Baking: 5
- Dairy: 1
- Eggs: 4
- Bread / Bakery: 1

Outside the home market, the national pool is workable but adds freight cost, cold-chain complexity, and lead time.

The roadmap therefore takes a **two-track approach for Wave A**: Midwest-first for produce and eggs (fast, fresh, low freight), and a single national meat partner aggregating soul-food cuts (slower, weekly freight, higher per-unit cost). Wave B requires significant **net-new vendor discovery** because regional dairy and bread are essentially absent from the DB. Wave C is brand-curation work; the supplier pool is fine. Wave D needs net-new vendor work.

Total capital ask fits **$185K-$235K**, inside the $250K SAFE-funded inventory line. Black-owned vendor coverage starts at ~70-80% by intent of the DB (it is the merged result of the legacy Black Farms + Black Vendors tables) and is held there in the Wave A and Wave C recommendation. Wave B and Wave D drop to ~40-50% Black-owned because regional dairy, bread, and frozen suppliers are mostly market-standard.

## 2. Wave B: universal weekly core (target 4-6 weeks)

The universal staples are where the supplier DB is **weakest** because the legacy Black Farms / Black Vendors merge skewed toward specialty / soul-food / urban-farm suppliers, not commodity-grocery suppliers. Wave B requires the most net-new vendor work.

### Categories that need net-new vendor discovery

- **Dairy (whole milk, cheese, yogurt, butter):** 40 in DB, only **1 regional with email** (Lee Hemp Farms, WI, which is hemp + dairy adjunct, not a commodity dairy). For commodity milk, Anthony will need to either (a) partner with an existing Chicago independent dairy (Oberweis, Kilgus Farmstead, Hartzler), or (b) accept that whole milk is sourced through a wholesale distributor (UNFI, KeHE, Caputo's, Mariano's wholesale program). The brand cost of (b) is one of the few places we yield to "market-standard" sourcing. Recommend (b) for milk and (a) for cheese where local Chicago artisan options exist.
- **Bread / Bakery (sandwich loaves, artisan):** 1 regional candidate in DB (Eden Place Farms, which is a farm not a bakery). Net-new vendor discovery required. Pick a Chicago independent bakery partner. Hewn (Evanston), Publican Quality Bread, or Bennison's are candidates; Phase 4 outreach has to add them to the Suppliers DB.
- **Bananas, citrus, off-season produce (apples, berries, avocados, cilantro, etc.):** DB has 315 Produce suppliers but most are seasonal Midwest farms. Year-round commodity produce will need a wholesale partner (Mariano's wholesale, US Foods, Sysco). Same brand-cost trade as milk. Mitigation: position Uncle May's bananas as "Equal Exchange fair-trade" or similar to maintain the brand promise, even if the supplier is a wholesale distributor.
- **Ground beef:** Wave A meat plan covers a national partner; ground beef is a shared SKU.
