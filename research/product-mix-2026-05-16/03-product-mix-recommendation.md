## TL;DR

Three tables follow: **Quick wins** (already in the catalog, market harder), **Source and add** (missing, source from existing supplier base), **Skip** (in the lists but conflicts with brand or ops).

Then a **phased launch sequence** that opens the soul-food basket first to use the differentiation Numerator already proved, then layers in the universal weekly-trip core, then snacks, then frozen/prepared. Working capital fits inside the $250K inventory envelope from the SAFE raise.

The single most important framing point: the current catalog is built around chef-y, single-origin, scarce produce (ramps, sunchokes, microgreens, black garlic, alpine radishes). That is a great cooking-class roster and a bad weekly-grocery roster. The fix is not to delete those SKUs. The fix is to put them in a "specialty" rail underneath a much larger "staples" rail. The CEO's instinct was right.

## 4. Phased launch sequence

The sequence below uses Tier-1 supplier coverage to ship the Black-household differentiator first (the merchandising thesis), then layers in the universal staples, then snacks, then frozen and prepared.

| Phase | Items | Ops complexity | Working-capital order-of-magnitude | Calendar |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Wave A, soul-food basket | Tier 2a above, ~11 SKUs | Low. Mostly same-channel adds to existing Produce / Meat / Pantry vendors. | $25K-$40K initial inventory. | 2-3 weeks from Phase 4 outreach start to first sale. |
| Wave B, universal weekly core | Tier 2b above, ~15 SKUs | Medium. Requires Dairy, Bread, and Eggs cold-chain widening. Many new vendors. Wholesale-distributor staples sit alongside Black-farmed specialty, with per-SKU "Black-owned" badge for honesty. | $60K-$90K initial inventory. | 4-6 weeks. |
| Wave C, snacks and condiments | Tier 2c above, ~5 SKU lines | Medium. Brand curation is the hard part, not sourcing. | $20K-$35K initial inventory. | 6-8 weeks. Can run in parallel with Wave B. |
| Wave D, frozen and prepared | Tier 2d above, ~6 SKU lines | High. Vendor discovery required for Frozen Foods (3 suppliers in DB) and Prepared Foods (17). | $40K-$70K initial inventory + cold-chain ops. | 10-14 weeks. |
| **Wave E, Black-owned personal care** | Soaps, oils, batana hair oil, skin care, hair care. 100% Black-owned by brand thesis. Source from Personal Care (39 in DB) + Health & Wellness (125). Batana specifically requires new vendor discovery. | Medium. Brand curation. Shelf-stable, freight-friendly. | $15K-$25K initial inventory. | 6-10 weeks. Can run in parallel with Wave C. |

**Total working-capital estimate with Wave E: $160K-$260K**, midpoint ~$210K. Inside the $250K SAFE-funded inventory line at midpoint; upper bound brushes 4% over and would force a Wave C or D trim. Order-of-magnitude only; firm-up requires Phase 4 vendor quotes. The estimate assumes 4-6 weeks of forward stock per SKU at retail price points consistent with the existing 1.25× cost markup.

**Black-owned supplier coverage:** the Suppliers DB does not separately tag Black-owned status in the schema observed. Phase 4 should add a Black-owned flag during outreach so the recommended ordering can re-rank toward Black-owned vendors per category where supply allows. This is consistent with the brand promise and was a CEO emphasis in the original "Run A Way Buckers Club" framing.

## 5. CVR test ladder before un-pausing paid

When paid relaunches in Phase 6, the order of "what evidence proves the offer works" matters. Run the test ladder against organic and email traffic first, in this order:

1. Wave A live for **5 business days** on organic traffic. Galileo + Pixel measure: did add-to-cart rate on hero items rise vs the produce-only baseline?
2. Wave B live for **5 more business days**. Same measurement. Did average order value (AOV) rise? Did completed orders per visitor (CR) rise?
3. If both moved positively, un-pause Meta + Google with ad creative that reflects the broader assortment (per Plan Phase 6). If neither moved, the assortment is not the bottleneck and we need to re-examine the funnel (Baymard audit, mobile UX) before spending paid again.

This ladder costs zero paid dollars and produces evidence the board can sign off on for the relaunch budget.
